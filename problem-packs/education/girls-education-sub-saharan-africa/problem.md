# Girls Secondary Education Enrollment And Retention Barriers In Sub-Saharan Africa

## Problem Statement

Sub-Saharan Africa has the world's highest gender gap in secondary education. Only 43 percent of girls complete lower secondary versus 50 percent of boys, and in several countries completion rates are below 20 percent. The barriers are documented — school fees, early marriage, adolescent pregnancy, distance to school, menstrual health management, safety concerns — but which barriers dominate where is not systematically verified. Intervention targeting requires knowing where gaps are largest and what is driving them.

> Which sub-national girls secondary education enrollment and retention gaps are evidence-backed enough to support intervention targeting?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: UNESCO reports Sub-Saharan Africa has the highest gender gap in secondary education globally, with 43 percent of girls completing lower secondary versus 50 percent of boys, and rates below 20 percent in Sahel countries.
- Verified fact: DHS and MICS surveys provide sub-national data on school attendance by gender and age, with data on early marriage, adolescent pregnancy, and household wealth — the key barrier correlates.
- Verified fact: A 2020 systematic review found that eliminating school fees, providing cash transfers, and improving school safety had the strongest evidence for increasing girls secondary enrollment, but effect sizes varied substantially by context.
- Verified fact: Distance to secondary school is a binding constraint in many rural areas, with average travel times exceeding 60 minutes for secondary students in several countries.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether survey data can distinguish between never-enrolled and drop-out girls at sub-national scale.
- How menstrual health management, early marriage, and safety concerns interact as compound barriers.
- Whether interventions effective in one country context transfer to others with different cultural, economic, and infrastructure conditions.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, sub-national enrollment and retention gap analysis, barrier classification, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national analysis of girls secondary education gaps for at least two countries with barrier classification.

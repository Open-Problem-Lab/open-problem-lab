# Indoor Air Pollution Exposure From Solid Fuel Cooking In Sub-Saharan Africa

## Problem Statement

Indoor air pollution kills 700,000 people annually in Sub-Saharan Africa — more than malaria. Women cooking over open fires inhale PM2.5 concentrations routinely exceeding 500 µg/m³, seventy times WHO guidelines. But exposure estimation at sub-national scale relies on fuel-type surveys that don't capture ventilation, cooking duration, or the near-universal practice of fuel stacking where households use both clean and solid fuels. Without more accurate exposure estimates, clean-cooking interventions risk targeting the wrong households.

> Which sub-national indoor PM2.5 exposure estimates from solid fuel cooking in Sub-Saharan Africa are evidence-backed enough to support clean-cooking intervention targeting?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO estimates 700,000 annual premature deaths in Sub-Saharan Africa from household air pollution, predominantly among women and children under five.
- Verified fact: DHS surveys capture primary cooking fuel type but do not measure indoor PM2.5 concentrations, cooking duration, or kitchen ventilation.
- Verified fact: Fuel stacking — simultaneous use of both clean and solid fuels — is practiced by 50-80 percent of households in clean-fuel programs, substantially reducing modeled health benefits.
- Verified fact: A 2022 review found that kitchen PM2.5 concentrations vary 10-fold between households using the same fuel type due to ventilation, stove condition, and cooking practices.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether fuel-type surveys combined with ventilation and duration parameters can produce exposure estimates accurate enough for intervention targeting.
- How fuel stacking affects population-level exposure estimates when survey data captures primary fuel only.
- Whether sub-national exposure maps change clean-cooking program decisions.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, household exposure estimation methodology, sub-national exposure mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national indoor air pollution exposure analysis for at least two countries with explicit fuel-stacking caveats.

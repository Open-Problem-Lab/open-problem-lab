# Menstrual Health Management Access And School Attendance Gaps In Low-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

Build a verified workflow for measuring menstrual health management access gaps and their association with school attendance in low-income countries.

> Which evidence-backed signals are sufficient to support intervention targeting without overstating certainty?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO/UNICEF JMP estimates millions of girls lack access to menstrual health materials, facilities, and information, with school absenteeism during menstruation reported by 10-20 percent of girls in multiple LMICs.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether available data is sufficient for sub-national analysis. Whether gap maps change actual policy or investment decisions. How measurement methodology compares across countries.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, dataset compilation, sub-national gap analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national analysis with explicit uncertainty documentation.

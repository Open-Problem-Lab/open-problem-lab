# Validation

## Validation Layers

| Layer           | Gate                                                                                 |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Structure       | `pnpm validate` passes schemas and problem-pack completeness checks                  |
| Evidence        | Every claim has a dated evidence record tagged by etiology hypothesis                |
| Reproducibility | Exposure-outcome syntheses include rerunnable ranking criteria                       |
| Review          | Required reviewer roles approve or request changes, with nephrology domain expertise |
| Replication     | Causal inference claims are independently replicated by a second reviewer            |

## Case Definition Requirements

Any CKDnt prevalence, incidence, or mortality claim must document:

- The case definition used (inclusion criteria, CKD exclusion criteria).
- The kidney function threshold (eGFR cut-off, biomarker, or clinical diagnosis).
- The population screening method (community survey, occupational screening, vital statistics).
- Whether conventional CKD causes (diabetes, hypertension, glomerulonephritis) were excluded.

## Exposure Assessment Requirements

Any exposure-outcome claim must state:

- Whether exposure was measured at the individual level or ecologically.
- The exposure metric (WBGT, temperature, pesticide concentration, water contaminant level).
- The temporal relationship between exposure and outcome measurement.
- Which confounders were measured and which were not.
- Whether co-exposures (heat and agrochemicals) were assessed simultaneously.

## Causal Inference Requirements

No causal claim may be accepted without:

- The strongest study design in the evidence base named.
- The causal inference level stated (association, dose-response, temporality, replication).
- A statement of what the evidence cannot prove at the current study design.
- A kill condition: the specific observation that would weaken or falsify the claim.

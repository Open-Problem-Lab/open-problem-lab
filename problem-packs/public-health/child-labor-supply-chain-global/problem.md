# Child Labor Detection And Supply Chain Risk In Global Agriculture

## Problem Statement

160 million children are in child labor, 70 percent in agriculture. EU and US forced-labor import bans create regulatory pressure on cocoa, coffee, cotton, and palm oil supply chains. But sub-national data on where child labor occurs is fragmentary — household surveys are infrequent, and linking survey data to specific crop production areas and supply-chain actors is methodologically underdeveloped.

> Which sub-national agricultural production areas have the highest evidence-backed child labor risk, and which supply chains are most implicated?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: ILO and UNICEF estimate 160 million children in child labor globally, 70 percent in agriculture, with the highest absolute numbers in Sub-Saharan Africa and highest prevalence in low-income agricultural households.
- Verified fact: EU Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive and US Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act create binding supply-chain traceability requirements for imported agricultural commodities.
- Verified fact: Multiple countries conduct child labor modules in national labor-force surveys, but survey frequency is low (every 5-10 years), sample sizes are inadequate for sub-national estimation, and survey definitions of child labor vary.
- Verified fact: Crop-production maps from satellite data (e.g., MapSPAM, OneSoil) can be linked to survey-based child labor prevalence to estimate supply-chain risk.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether survey-based child labor prevalence can be validly extrapolated to unsurveyed areas using crop-type proxies. Whether supply-chain traceability data is accessible enough for risk mapping. Whether risk maps change corporate sourcing or government enforcement behavior.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, child labor survey compilation, crop-production mapping, supply-chain risk analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national child-labor supply-chain risk analysis for at least two agricultural commodities and two countries.

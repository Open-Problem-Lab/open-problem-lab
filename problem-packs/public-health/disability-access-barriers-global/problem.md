# Disability Access Barriers In LMICs

## Problem Statement

1.3 billion people — 16 percent of the world — live with significant disability. 80 percent are in LMICs where health and education access is already constrained. The Washington Group questions in DHS and census surveys now enable disability-disaggregated analysis. But geographic mapping of where disabled populations face the greatest compounded barriers to health facilities and schools is underdeveloped.

> Which sub-national areas in LMICs have the highest evidence-backed compounded disability access barriers to health and education services?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO estimates 1.3 billion people have significant disability, 80 percent in LMICs, with disability prevalence rising due to population aging and non-communicable diseases.
- Verified fact: The Washington Group Short Set questions are now included in DHS and MICS surveys in over 70 countries, enabling sub-national disability-prevalence estimation for the first time at scale.
- Verified fact: A 2022 systematic review found that disabled children in LMICs are 30-50 percent less likely to attend school than non-disabled peers, and disabled adults face 2-3 times higher barriers to healthcare access.
- Verified fact: Facility-accessibility audits are rare in LMICs, and most health and education facility registries lack any accessibility indicators.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether Washington Group survey data has sufficient sample sizes for sub-national disability estimation. Whether facility-accessibility data can be collected at scale without on-site audits. Whether access-barrier maps change inclusive-service planning.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, disability-prevalence mapping, health-facility access analysis, school-access analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national disability-access-barrier analysis for at least two LMICs.

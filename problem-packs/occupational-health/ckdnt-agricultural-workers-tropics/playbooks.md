# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Classify every source by etiology hypothesis before pooling evidence.
4. Do not mix evidence from different case definitions without explicit comparison.
5. Mark every causal claim with its evidence level and kill condition.
6. Do not make policy recommendations that exceed the evidence base.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask six questions:

1. What causal claim is being made?
2. Which etiology hypothesis does it support?
3. What study design is the claim based on?
4. What would make the claim false?
5. Does the claim distinguish individual-level from ecological exposure?
6. Who could misuse this claim to justify premature policy action?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether the evidence synthesis is impressive. Ask whether it would change a named decision for a labor minister, agricultural extension director, occupational health agency, or nephrology clinician in an affected country, at a useful time, without creating worse incentives for affected worker communities.

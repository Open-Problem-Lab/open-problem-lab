## Evidence Notes

### Murray et al. 2022 (Lancet)

The most cited AMR burden study. Estimated 1.27 million direct deaths and 4.95 million associated deaths in 2019. SSA and South Asia carry the highest burden and lowest surveillance capacity. Use this as the reference burden estimate for surveillance-gap analysis. Publisher page blocks automated access; DOI and archive URL provided as fallback.

### WHO GLASS Program Page

Official WHO source for GLASS system description, enrollment scope, and data products. Page describes the surveillance system but does not provide downloadable sub-national data. Use this for program context and enrollment framing. GLASS reports (separate publications) contain enrollment counts and quality metrics.

### WHO AMR Fact Sheet

Official WHO summary of global AMR burden and surveillance rationale. Burden figures cite Murray et al. 2022; fact sheet is a secondary presentation. Use for framing, not for data extraction. HTTP last-modified header confirmed page update date.

### IHME GBD Compare

Country-level AMR burden estimates through an interactive visualization tool. Useful for cross-country comparison but carries wide uncertainty for low-surveillance countries. No sub-national grain. Estimates share modeling assumptions with Murray et al. Use for burden comparison, not for surveillance-gap measurement.

### Global-PPS

Hospital-level antimicrobial prescribing data from 50+ countries. LMIC hospital participation is limited. Covers prescribing patterns, not resistance rates. Use for consumption context and quality-of-prescribing analysis, not for resistance surveillance-gap mapping.

### ResistanceMap (One Health Trust)

Country-level resistance rates for key pathogen-drug combinations from 2000 onward. LMIC data is sparse and may be outdated. Heterogeneous surveillance methodologies make cross-country comparison imprecise. Use for exploratory comparison, not for definitive gap measurement.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

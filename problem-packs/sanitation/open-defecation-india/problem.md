# Open Defecation Elimination And Sanitation Coverage Verification In India

## Problem Statement

India's Swachh Bharat Mission built over 100 million toilets and declared the country open-defecation-free in 2019. Independent surveys tell a more complicated story — the National Family Health Survey found that substantial proportions of households in several states continued open defecation. The gap between administrative claims and measured reality is not an accounting problem. It is a public-health emergency. Unsafe sanitation contributes to an estimated 200,000 child diarrheal deaths annually in India. Where is the data gap largest, and what would it take to close it honestly?

> Which district-level sanitation coverage estimates and open-defecation measurements are evidence-backed enough to support honest targeting of remaining sanitation gaps?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO and UNICEF Joint Monitoring Programme data shows India eliminated an estimated 450 million open defecators between 2015 and 2020, the fastest sanitation improvement in recorded history, but still has over 150 million people practicing open defecation by independent estimates.
- Verified fact: The National Family Health Survey-5 found that open defecation persists in 10-30 percent of households in several large states despite official ODF declarations.
- Verified fact: Unsafe sanitation causes an estimated 200,000 diarrheal deaths among children under five annually in India, concentrated in states with the largest sanitation access gaps.
- Verified fact: Toilet construction does not guarantee use — multiple studies find usage rates 15-30 percentage points below construction rates, driven by water access, maintenance, caste norms, and behavioral factors.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether administrative toilet-construction data can be reconciled with independent household survey data at district scale.
- How toilet use versus construction can be verified at scale without expensive spot-check surveys.
- Whether district-level child health improvements can be causally attributed to sanitation coverage gains versus other concurrent interventions.
- How behavioral and caste-based factors interact with infrastructure availability to sustain open defecation.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on: evidence inventory, dataset availability including NFHS, SBM administrative data, census data, and child health data, district-level sanitation coverage triangulation, toilet-use versus construction gap analysis, health outcome correlation, red-team review, field-reality review.

This pack does not accept sanitation program recommendations, toilet construction targets, or unvalidated open-defecation rate claims.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated district-level sanitation coverage analysis that triangulates administrative, survey, and health data, with explicit data-conflict documentation, that a domain reviewer judges suitable for program discussion rather than target-setting.

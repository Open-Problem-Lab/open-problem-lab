# Snakebite Antivenom Access Gaps

## Problem Statement

5.4 million snakebites annually cause 138,000 deaths and 400,000 amputations. SSA and South Asia carry 85 percent. Antivenom is the only effective treatment, but stock-outs are common and counterfeits widespread. Mapping snakebite incidence against facility-level antivenom stocking at district scale is the missing operational tool.

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO estimates 5.4 million snakebites, 138,000 deaths, 400,000 amputations annually. SSA and South Asia carry 85 percent. Agricultural workers and children are at highest risk.
- Verified fact: WHO added snakebite envenoming to the neglected tropical disease list in 2017 and launched a global strategy targeting 50 percent mortality reduction by 2030.
- Verified fact: Antivenom stock-outs are reported in over 60 percent of high-burden districts in multiple countries, and counterfeit or inappropriate antivenoms are common.
- Verified fact: Agricultural population maps and snakebite incidence surveys exist but have not been linked to facility-level antivenom stocking data.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether snakebite incidence can be modeled from agricultural population and snake-habitat proxies where surveys are absent. Whether antivenom supply-chain data is accessible.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national antivenom access gap analysis for at least two high-burden countries.

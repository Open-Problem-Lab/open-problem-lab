## Evidence Notes

### WHO Global Tobacco Epidemic Report 2024

Use this source for the global tobacco mortality burden (8 million deaths annually, 80 percent in LMICs) and the MPOWER policy-implementation data showing that only 41 of 195 countries have tobacco taxes exceeding 75 percent of retail price — the WHO FCTC recommended minimum. The tax-policy data is self-reported by member states. Do not use the MPOWER taxation indicator alone as proof of effective tax policy — it measures existence, not enforcement or impact on consumption.

### Illicit Trade Context

The WHO Protocol to Eliminate Illicit Trade in Tobacco Products estimates that illicit trade accounts for 10-15 percent of the global cigarette market, but country-level estimates vary widely and are frequently inflated by the tobacco industry to oppose tax increases. Any tax-prevalence analysis must include explicit discussion of how illicit-trade estimates affect modeled tax-impact projections and must distinguish between industry-funded and independent illicit-trade studies.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

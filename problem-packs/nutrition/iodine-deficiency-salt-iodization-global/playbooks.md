# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. Document UIC methodology, salt testing protocol, survey year, and geographic grain for every iodine status or coverage estimate.
5. Keep quantitative outputs reproducible.
6. Mark every intervention recommendation as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made?
2. What source proves it?
3. What method produced it?
4. What would make it false?
5. Who could misuse it?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a coverage map is impressive. Ask whether it changes a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For iodine deficiency and salt iodization, the named users are UNICEF country office nutrition section chiefs, national IDD elimination committees, salt producer associations, and the Iodine Global Network. The misuse risk is justifying program abandonment in countries with stagnating coverage, defaming small-scale salt producers, shifting iodization costs to consumers through price increases, or discrediting salt iodization as a strategy based on partial coverage data.

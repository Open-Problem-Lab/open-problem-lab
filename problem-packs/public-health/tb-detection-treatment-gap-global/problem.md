# TB Detection And Treatment Gaps In High-Burden Countries

## Problem Statement

TB killed 1.3 million people in 2023. WHO estimates 4 million of the 10.6 million incident cases went undiagnosed or unreported. The private sector treats up to 50 percent of patients in India, Indonesia, Philippines, and Pakistan but doesn't systematically report to national programs. Sub-national detection-gap maps that include private-sector notifications are essential for targeting active case finding.

> Which sub-national areas in high-burden countries have the highest evidence-backed TB detection and treatment completion gaps?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO Global TB Report 2024 estimates 10.6 million incident TB cases, 1.3 million deaths, and 4 million undiagnosed or unreported cases, with India, Indonesia, China, Philippines, and Pakistan carrying 60 percent of the global burden.
- Verified fact: Private-sector TB care accounts for 40-50 percent of initial care-seeking in several high-burden countries, but private notifications to national programs are incomplete despite mandatory notification policies.
- Verified fact: National TB prevalence surveys are conducted approximately every 10 years and provide the only direct measurement of the detection gap, but sub-national breakdowns have wide confidence intervals.
- Verified fact: TB treatment success rates globally are 85 percent, but drop below 70 percent in drug-resistant TB and in patients lost to follow-up, with sub-national variation poorly documented.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether private-sector notification data is complete enough for sub-national detection-gap estimation. Whether TB prevalence surveys can be complemented with routine notification data for more frequent gap estimates. Whether detection-gap maps change active-case-finding strategy.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, TB notification data compilation, private-sector linkage, detection-gap mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national TB detection gap analysis for at least two high-burden countries.

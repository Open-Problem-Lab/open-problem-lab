# NTD MDA Coverage Gaps In Sub-Saharan Africa

## Problem Statement

1.7 billion people need mass drug administration for NTDs annually. LF, oncho, schisto, STH, and trachoma are preventable with donated drugs. WHO tracks coverage at district level through the PCT databank. But coverage data is self-reported by national programs with variable quality, and disease-mapping surveys to verify elimination are conducted infrequently. The gap between reported MDA coverage and verified disease persistence is the critical measurement for elimination targeting.

> Which districts in Sub-Saharan Africa have the highest evidence-backed gap between reported MDA coverage and verified disease persistence?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO estimates 1.7 billion people require MDA for at least one NTD annually, with Sub-Saharan Africa carrying the highest burden for all five major PCT diseases.
- Verified fact: WHO PCT databank tracks MDA coverage at district level with 70 percent of endemic districts reporting, but data quality is self-reported and coverage figures above 100 percent are reported in some districts indicating denominator errors.
- Verified fact: Disease-mapping surveys for LF, oncho, and trachoma show that reported MDA coverage frequently overestimates actual treatment coverage by 20-40 percentage points when compared to coverage surveys.
- Verified fact: The 2030 NTD Roadmap targets elimination of at least one NTD in 100 countries, but elimination verification requires transmission-assessment surveys that are not conducted in all endemic districts.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether coverage data quality can be assessed remotely without coverage surveys. How treatment-register data can validate reported coverage. Whether coverage-gap maps change MDA strategy.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, coverage data compilation, disease-mapping comparison, gap identification, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated district-level MDA coverage-gap analysis for at least two countries and two NTDs.

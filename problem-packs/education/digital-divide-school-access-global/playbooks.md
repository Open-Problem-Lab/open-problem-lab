# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. For connectivity mapping: always document the classification methodology — satellite-derived (Giga), school census self-report, or mobile-network proxy. Document data currency and cross-validation status between sources.
5. For gap analysis: always document the quality dimension — connectivity existence does not equal educational utility. Electricity, devices, teacher digital literacy, and content are all necessary conditions.
6. For classification: always document that satellite-based classification may misclassify intermittent or mobile-only connectivity — a school with sporadic mobile coverage is not equivalent to one with reliable broadband.
7. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made about school connectivity or the digital divide?
2. What source proves it — and is it Giga satellite classification, school census self-report, or mobile-network coverage data?
3. What method classifies schools as connected vs. unconnected, and what is the cross-validation status?
4. What would make it false — particularly if satellite classification misclassifies intermittent connectivity, if school census data is outdated, if connectivity doesn't translate to educational use without complementary investments?
5. Who could misuse it — particularly to drive technology procurement without electricity, devices, and teacher training, or to present connectivity maps as digital-readiness assessments?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a connectivity map is impressive. Ask whether it would change a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For school connectivity in low-income countries, ask specifically:

- Would a Rwanda Ministry of Education ICT director use this to target school connectivity infrastructure, or would it drive technology procurement without complementary investments in electricity and teacher training?
- Would a Giga program manager use this to prioritize country engagement, or would satellite classification be treated as ground-truth connectivity when it may misclassify intermittent access?
- Would a connectivity funder use this to allocate resources, or would it ignore that connectivity without devices and digital literacy does not close the divide?

The answer must include explicit non-use cases before any field-facing output is published.

## Evidence Notes

### Africa Fertilizer Summit 2023

Use this source for the continental fertilizer-use baseline. The 15 kg/ha is a continental average — individual countries range from under 5 to over 50 kg/ha. The 3 percent GDP cost is modeled from research-site nutrient-balance studies. Sub-national nutrient data requires interpolation. Always document country-specific variation and data-source limitations.

### AfSIS Soil Nutrient Depletion

Use AfSIS for soil-nutrient data with the critical caveat that coverage is concentrated at research sites — spatial interpolation introduces uncertainty. Nutrient depletion rates require repeated measurements available at very few sites. Nutrient-balance calculations depend on crop-yield and fertilizer data that varies in quality. Always document research-site extrapolation limitations.

### Fertilizer Distribution Infrastructure

Use this source for distribution-barrier documentation. The 50-200 percent markup range is from limited country studies — actual markups vary by season, type, and location. Distribution infrastructure is not systematically mapped. Informal channels may serve remote areas outside formal supply chains. Always present with country-specific and seasonal context.

### Fertilizer Response Degraded Soils

Use this source for soil-response variability documentation. The 30-50 percent response reduction is pooled across diverse soil types and crops. On-farm response differs from research-station trials. Response rates change over time with sustained application. Always present with soil-type and crop specificity.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

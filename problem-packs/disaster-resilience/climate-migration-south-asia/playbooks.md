# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. For migration-data compilation: always document census migration definitions used by each country and the harmonization methodology — place-of-birth, last-residence, and duration-of-residence definitions produce different migration counts.
5. For environmental correlation: always document economic confound controls, variance-explained percentage (typically 5-15 percent), and explicitly state that correlation does not imply causation — environmental signals may proxy for agricultural income loss rather than causing migration directly.
6. For attribution: always present environmental-migration relationships as modest correlations with marginal explanatory power, not as evidence of climate-driven displacement.
7. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made about climate-driven migration?
2. What source proves it — and is it a census correlation, a modeled projection (like Groundswell), or an observed migration flow?
3. What method separates environmental from economic drivers, and what is the variance-explained by environmental signals?
4. What would make it false — particularly if economic confounders explain the correlation, if census definitions don't capture the migration type of interest, or if the environmental signal proxies for income loss rather than causing migration?
5. Who could misuse it — particularly to restrict mobility, to justify climate-attribution claims without economic confound controls, or to present modest correlations as dominant climate-driven displacement?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a migration-environment correlation map is impressive. Ask whether it would change a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For climate migration in South Asia, ask specifically:

- Would a Dhaka city urban planner use this to plan service provision for climate-origin migrants, or would it be used to restrict mobility rather than support adaptation?
- Would an adaptation policymaker use this to target agricultural resilience programs in high-out-migration districts, or would it overstate climate causation when economic drivers are more important?
- Would a district administrator use this to prepare for population change, or would modest correlations (5-15 percent) be presented as dominant climate-driven displacement?

The answer must include explicit non-use cases before any field-facing output is published.

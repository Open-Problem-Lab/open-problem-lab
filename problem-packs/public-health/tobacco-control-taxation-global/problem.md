# Tobacco Taxation And Smoking In LMICs

## Problem Statement

Tobacco kills 8 million annually, 80 percent in LMICs. WHO FCTC recommends 75 percent tax share, but most LMICs are below 50 percent. The industry claims higher taxes fuel illicit trade. STEPS surveys measure sub-national smoking prevalence. Linking tax policy, prevalence, and illicit trade data would enable evidence-based taxation — but this linkage is rarely done at sub-national scale.

> In which LMICs and sub-national areas would tobacco tax increases have the largest evidence-backed impact on smoking prevalence, accounting for illicit trade?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO reports tobacco kills 8 million people annually, 80 percent in LMICs, and tobacco taxes are the single most effective tobacco control measure.
- Verified fact: WHO Global Tobacco Control Report tracks MPOWER policy implementation including taxation, finding that only 41 countries have taxes above 75 percent of retail price.
- Verified fact: STEPS surveys and GATS provide sub-national smoking prevalence data in over 100 countries.
- Verified fact: Illicit tobacco trade accounts for 10-15 percent of the global market, concentrated in countries with weak enforcement, but industry estimates are frequently inflated.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether tax-increase impact models transfer across countries. Whether illicit trade estimates are reliable.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, tax-policy compilation, prevalence mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed tax-prevalence gap analysis for at least two LMICs.

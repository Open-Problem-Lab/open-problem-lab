# Perinatal Maternal Mental Health In Low-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

10-20 percent of women in LMICs experience perinatal depression. Detection rates are below 10 percent and treatment near zero. Maternal depression affects child growth, development, and survival through reduced caregiving, breastfeeding, and health-seeking. Yet maternal mental health is excluded from most maternal health programs, and sub-national data on the detection-treatment gap is absent.

> Which sub-national areas have the highest evidence-backed gap between perinatal depression prevalence and detection-treatment access?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO estimates 10-20 percent of women in LMICs experience perinatal depression, with rates up to 25 percent in conflict-affected areas. Detection is below 10 percent in most low-income countries.
- Verified fact: The Thinking Healthy Programme and similar task-sharing approaches using community health workers for maternal depression have demonstrated effectiveness in multiple LMIC trials.
- Verified fact: Maternal depression is associated with 30-50 percent increased risk of child stunting and 20-40 percent reduced completion of child immunization schedules.
- Verified fact: DHS surveys in several countries include maternal depression screening modules, enabling sub-national prevalence estimation.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether screening tools validated in one context are valid in others. Whether task-sharing interventions effective in trials scale to national programs. Whether detection-gap maps change maternal mental health program investment.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, DHS maternal mental health data compilation, detection-gap mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national maternal mental health detection gap analysis for at least two LMICs.

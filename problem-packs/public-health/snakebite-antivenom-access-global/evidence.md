## Evidence Notes

### WHO Snakebite Envenoming Strategy 2019

Use this source for the global snakebite burden baseline — 5.4 million bites, 138,000 deaths, 400,000 amputations annually — and the WHO strategy targeting 50 percent mortality reduction by 2030. The burden estimates are modeled from surveys conducted in a limited number of high-burden countries. Snakebite incidence is systematically underreported because most victims seek care from traditional healers rather than health facilities. Do not use these global estimates as a substitute for sub-national incidence data where available.

### Antivenom Access Context

Antivenom is the only effective treatment for snakebite envenoming and is on the WHO Essential Medicines List. However, stock-outs are reported in over 60 percent of high-burden districts in multiple countries, and inappropriate or counterfeit antivenoms — manufactured for species from other regions — are widespread. Any antivenom access-gap analysis must distinguish between reported antivenom-stocking data (which may record expired or inappropriate products as "available") and functional antivenom access as measured by treatment outcomes.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

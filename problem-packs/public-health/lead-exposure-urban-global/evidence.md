## Evidence Notes

### UNICEF/Pure Earth Toxic Truth 2020

Use this source as the landmark evidence for global childhood lead burden and as the basis for exposure pathway categorization. Do not use it to assert lead levels for a specific neighborhood.

### WHO Lead Poisoning Fact Sheet

Use this source for the health-effects consensus, the no-safe-level finding, and the mortality burden estimates. The cardiovascular mortality pathway is important context but not this pack's spatial focus.

### Ericson et al. 2021 Kathmandu

Use this source as the strongest direct evidence that informal recycling creates hyperlocal, neighborhood-scale lead hotspots measurable in soil. Treat findings as a proof-of-concept for spatial risk mapping, not as a validated model for other cities.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

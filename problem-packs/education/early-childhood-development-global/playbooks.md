# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. For ECD-outcome mapping: always document MICS ECDI instrument comparability across countries and survey rounds — instruments have been revised and parent-reported milestones vary across cultural contexts.
5. For intervention-coverage analysis: always document the coverage-vs-impact distinction — enrollment in preschool or participation in parenting programs does not equal verified developmental impact without quality and dosage data.
6. For effectiveness claims: always present parenting-intervention effects with heterogeneity and sustainability documentation — 0.3-0.5 SD is from small-scale trials that may not replicate at program scale.
7. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made about ECD outcomes or intervention coverage?
2. What source proves it — and is it MICS ECDI data, preschool enrollment from EMIS, or a parenting-program trial?
3. What method translates intervention coverage into developmental-impact claims, and what is the quality and dosage documentation?
4. What would make it false — particularly if ECDI instruments vary across countries, if preschool enrollment doesn't capture quality, if parenting-intervention trials don't replicate at scale, or if sustainability beyond program period is unknown?
5. Who could misuse it — particularly to claim developmental impact from coverage data, to overgeneralize trial results to program scale, or to ignore ECD-measurement comparability limitations?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether an ECD-outcome map is impressive. Ask whether it would change a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For ECD in LMICs, ask specifically:

- Would a UNICEF Ethiopia ECD program officer use this to target preschool expansion, or would coverage data be treated as developmental-impact evidence without quality data?
- Would an ECD ministry use this to prioritize parenting-program investment, or would trial effect sizes be assumed to hold at program scale without replication evidence?
- Would a nutrition funder use this to justify integrated ECD-nutrition programming, or would it ignore that ECD-measurement instruments vary across contexts?

The answer must include explicit non-use cases before any field-facing output is published.

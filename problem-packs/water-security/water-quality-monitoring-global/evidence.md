## Evidence Notes

### WHO/UNICEF JMP Progress Report 2024

Use this source for the drinking-water quality monitoring baseline — 2 billion people drink water contaminated with feces (E. coli detected in the source or at the point of use). Fewer than 30 countries report sub-national E. coli or thermotolerant coliform data to JMP. Fewer than 15 countries have conducted nationally representative water-quality surveys that include microbial testing at the household level.

The JMP estimates for water quality are modeled for countries without testing data, using regression-based predictions from water-source type and sanitation coverage. These modeled estimates smooth substantial within-country variation and cannot identify local contamination hotspots. Do not use the JMP 2-billion figure as a substitute for sub-national water-quality monitoring-gap analysis.

### Water-Point Data and Environmental Risk Proxies

The Water Point Data Exchange (WPDx), Akvo Flow, and mWater platforms collectively contain millions of geo-located water-point records with functionality status and, in some cases, water-quality test results. These datasets can supplement JMP data for sub-national monitoring-gap analysis but have substantial biases — water points in NGO program areas are overrepresented, and test frequency varies dramatically. Satellite-derived environmental risk factors (proximity to open defecation, flood risk, livestock density) provide contamination-risk proxies for areas without testing data but must be validated against actual water-quality measurements.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

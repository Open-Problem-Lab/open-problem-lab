# Coral Bleaching Detection And Reef Recovery Tracking In The Great Barrier Reef

## Problem Statement

The Great Barrier Reef has bleached six times since 1998, five times in the last eight years. NOAA's Coral Reef Watch provides satellite-based bleaching alerts using degree-heating-week thresholds. But satellite SST measures water surface temperature — it doesn't see what happens underwater. In-water surveys by AIMS and reef operators provide ground truth but cover a fraction of the 2,300 km reef system. The question is how to combine satellite thermal stress data with in-water bleaching observations to verify bleaching severity and track recovery at reef scale.

> Which reef-scale bleaching severity and recovery signals are evidence-backed enough by combined satellite and in-water data to support reef management discussion?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: The GBR has experienced six mass bleaching events since 1998, with events in 2016, 2017, 2020, 2022, and 2024 — five of the six occurring since 2016.
- Verified fact: NOAA Coral Reef Watch provides degree-heating-week alerts at 5km resolution that correctly identify bleaching thermal stress, but satellite SST can differ from in-situ reef-flat temperature by 1-3°C.
- Verified fact: AIMS Long-Term Monitoring Program surveys approximately 100 reefs annually, providing the most comprehensive in-water bleaching and recovery data but covering under 3 percent of GBR reefs.
- Verified fact: Recovery depends on bleaching severity, species composition, local water quality, and time between events — with repeat bleaching before recovery causing cumulative mortality.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether satellite DHW thresholds calibrated globally are accurate for GBR-specific bleaching sensitivity.
- How in-water survey coverage gaps can be filled with satellite-derived bleaching estimates without overstatement.
- Whether recovery can be reliably tracked from satellite-derived coral cover proxies.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, satellite bleaching alert validation against AIMS surveys, recovery classification, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated reef-scale bleaching detection and recovery analysis for at least ten GBR reefs.

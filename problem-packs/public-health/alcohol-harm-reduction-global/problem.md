# Alcohol Harm Reduction Policy Gaps In LMICs

## Problem Statement

Alcohol kills 3 million annually, 75 percent among men, and LMICs have the fastest-growing alcohol markets. WHO SAFER recommends taxation, marketing restrictions, and availability controls. But policy implementation is weakest where market growth is fastest. STEPS surveys capture sub-national heavy episodic drinking. Linking policy gaps to drinking prevalence identifies where harm reduction is most neglected.

> Which LMICs have the largest evidence-backed gap between alcohol harm reduction policy and heavy episodic drinking prevalence?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO reports 3 million alcohol-attributable deaths annually, 75 percent among men, with LMICs having the lowest policy implementation and fastest market growth.
- Verified fact: WHO Global Alcohol Report tracks SAFER policy implementation, finding that fewer than 40 LMICs have comprehensive alcohol taxation and marketing restrictions.
- Verified fact: STEPS surveys in 100+ countries measure heavy episodic drinking at sub-national scale.
- Verified fact: Alcohol industry in LMICs increasingly uses digital marketing and cross-border advertising that evades national regulations.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether policy-implementation indices capture actual enforcement. Whether survey data captures informal and home-brewed alcohol consumption.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, policy-prevalence gap mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed gap analysis for at least two LMICs.

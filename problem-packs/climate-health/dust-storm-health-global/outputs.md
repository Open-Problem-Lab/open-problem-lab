## Candidate Output Types

- Evidence inventory with satellite dust-detection product comparison and LMIC arid-region monitor coverage assessment.
- Sub-national dust-exposure map from satellite AOD and CAMS forecasts for at least two LMIC arid regions.
- Respiratory health-impact analysis with dust-source toxicology and non-dust PM10 confound controls.
- Failure-mode review of dust-exposure misclassification and health-impact overstatement.
- Field-reality review with air-quality management and health-ministry context.

## Publication Rule

Published outputs must say what they are not. A dust-health impact estimate is not a dust-suppression recommendation, a land-degradation policy basis, or a verified health-impact measurement for specific communities. AOD-based exposure estimates over bright desert surfaces have reduced accuracy — this limitation must be explicit in every output.

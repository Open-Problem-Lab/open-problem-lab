# Climate-Driven Internal Migration In South Asia

## Problem Statement

The World Bank estimates 40 million climate migrants in South Asia by 2050. Migration data comes from decadal censuses that ask where people live, not why they moved. Satellite data can see the droughts, floods, and crop failures that people flee. But linking environmental signals to migration flows without overstating causation is the core methodological challenge.

> Which sub-national areas in South Asia show the strongest evidence-backed correlation between environmental change and out-migration, with attribution appropriately qualified?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: World Bank Groundswell report estimates climate change could force 40 million internal migrants in South Asia by 2050, primarily from agricultural areas affected by water stress and crop failure.
- Verified fact: Census of India and other national censuses capture internal migration flows at district level but are conducted every 10 years, making near-real-time detection impossible from census data alone.
- Verified fact: Satellite-derived measures of environmental change — NDVI trends, rainfall anomalies, flood frequency — can be linked to census migration data at district scale, with a 2021 review finding moderate but significant correlations.
- Verified fact: Economic drivers (urban wage differentials, agricultural distress) and social networks explain more migration variance than environmental signals alone in most studies.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether environmental-migration correlations are causal or confounded by economic drivers. Whether migration is adaptive or distress-driven in specific regions. Whether migration-pattern maps change policy responses.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, census migration data compilation, environmental-change mapping, correlation analysis with attribution qualification, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated environmental-migration correlation analysis for at least two South Asian countries with explicit attribution caveats.

# Prison Health TB And HIV Service Gaps In LMICs

## Problem Statement

Prison TB rates are 10-100x the general population. HIV prevalence is 2-10x higher. Overcrowding and delayed diagnosis create transmission hotspots. Yet prison health is systematically excluded from national statistics in most LMICs. Mapping where prison populations have highest disease burden and lowest service access is the missing first step.

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO estimates prison TB notification rates 10-100 times higher than civilian rates, and prison HIV prevalence 2-10 times higher, driven by overcrowding, delayed diagnosis, and interrupted treatment.
- Verified fact: The Nelson Mandela Rules and UNODC mandate equivalence of prison health services with community standards, but prison health remains excluded from most national health information systems.
- Verified fact: A 2021 Lancet review found that fewer than 30 LMICs report any prison health data to WHO, and fewer than 10 disaggregate by prison facility.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, prison-population compilation, disease-burden estimation, service-gap analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated prison health service gap analysis for at least two LMICs.

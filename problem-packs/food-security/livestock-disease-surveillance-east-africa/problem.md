# Livestock Disease Surveillance And Pastoralist Livelihood Risk In East Africa

## Problem Statement

East African pastoralists manage 60 percent of the region's livestock and support 20 million people. A single Rift Valley fever outbreak can kill 90 percent of newborn lambs and kids. PPR and CBPP cause chronic losses that erode herd sizes over years. FAO's Global Animal Disease Information System and OIE's WAHIS collect outbreak reports, but linking reported outbreaks to satellite-derived forage conditions and market-price signals is the missing chain.

> Which East African pastoralist areas face the highest evidence-backed compound risk from livestock disease, forage stress, and market-price collapse?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: FAO estimates livestock contribute 30-50 percent of agricultural GDP in East African countries and support 20 million pastoralists whose livelihoods depend almost entirely on herd health.
- Verified fact: Rift Valley fever outbreaks in East Africa are associated with heavy rainfall and vegetation greening that creates mosquito breeding habitat, suggesting satellite-based early warning is feasible.
- Verified fact: The OIE WAHIS system collects country-reported disease outbreak data, but reporting is incomplete, delayed, and lacks sub-national spatial precision in many pastoralist areas.
- Verified fact: NDVI-based livestock insurance programs in Kenya and Ethiopia have demonstrated that satellite forage indices can predict livestock mortality at index scale.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether syndromic surveillance from community health workers can fill WAHIS reporting gaps at actionable speed, and whether disease-risk maps change veterinary service deployment or livestock market decisions.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, disease-surveillance data compilation, forage-condition analysis, market-price anomaly detection, risk mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated livestock disease-and-livelihood risk analysis for at least two East African countries.

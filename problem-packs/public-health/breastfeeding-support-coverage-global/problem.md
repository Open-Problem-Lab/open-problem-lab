# Early Breastfeeding Initiation And Exclusive Breastfeeding Support Coverage In Low-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

Breastfeeding could save 820,000 child lives annually — the single most effective child-survival intervention. Yet only 44 percent of newborns breastfeed within the first hour, and 48 percent are exclusively breastfed for six months. Baby-Friendly Hospital Initiative certification exists but sub-national linkage of facility certification, maternity-leave policy, and actual feeding practices is absent from health information systems.

> Which sub-national areas have the largest evidence-backed gaps between breastfeeding-support coverage and actual early and exclusive breastfeeding rates?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO/UNICEF estimate that universal breastfeeding could prevent 820,000 child deaths annually, yet global rates of early initiation (44 percent) and exclusive breastfeeding at six months (48 percent) remain far below universal coverage.
- Verified fact: The Baby-Friendly Hospital Initiative has certified over 20,000 facilities globally but certification coverage is highly uneven — many high-burden districts have no BFHI-certified facilities.
- Verified fact: DHS surveys capture early breastfeeding initiation and exclusive breastfeeding rates at cluster level, enabling sub-national analysis when linked to facility and policy data.
- Verified fact: Maternity-leave policies and workplace breastfeeding support are strong predictors of exclusive breastfeeding duration but are rarely mapped alongside feeding-practice data.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether DHS feeding-practice data can be linked to specific facility catchments at scale. Whether BFHI certification predicts actual breastfeeding-support quality. Whether coverage-gap maps change maternity-protection policy or facility-certification strategy.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, DHS breastfeeding data compilation, facility BFHI-certification mapping, maternity-policy gap analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national breastfeeding-support coverage gap analysis for at least two LMICs.

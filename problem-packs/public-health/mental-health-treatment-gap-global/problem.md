# Mental Health Treatment Gap Measurement In Low- And Middle-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

Depression is the leading cause of disability worldwide. The mental health treatment gap — the proportion of people with mental disorders receiving no care — exceeds 75 percent in LMICs and surpasses 90 percent for psychosis. But these are global estimates. Sub-national treatment gap measurement requires simultaneously knowing how many people have a condition and how many receive treatment — both numbers are poorly measured in the places where the gap is largest.

> Which sub-national mental health treatment gap estimates in LMICs are evidence-backed enough to support service planning?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO estimates the mental health treatment gap exceeds 75 percent in most LMICs and surpasses 90 percent for severe mental disorders, with depression the leading cause of disability globally.
- Verified fact: The World Mental Health Survey Initiative provides the most comparable cross-country prevalence data, but LMIC coverage is sparse and often limited to urban areas.
- Verified fact: Mental health workforce density in low-income countries averages 0.1 psychiatrists and 0.3 psychiatric nurses per 100,000 population — 200 times lower than in high-income countries.
- Verified fact: Task-sharing approaches using community health workers for depression care have demonstrated effectiveness in multiple LMIC trials, suggesting that treatment gap measurement must account for non-specialist care.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether household survey instruments are culturally valid across diverse LMIC settings, how traditional and faith-based care should be counted in treatment coverage, and whether treatment gap maps change actual mental health service investment.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, prevalence data inventory, treatment coverage measurement, sub-national gap estimation, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national mental health treatment gap analysis for at least two LMICs.

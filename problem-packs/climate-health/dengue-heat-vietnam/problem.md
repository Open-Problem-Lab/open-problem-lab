# Dengue, Heat, And Rainfall Risk In Viet Nam

## Problem Statement

Viet Nam needs a verified way to turn dengue, climate, water-infrastructure, and mobility evidence into scoped analytic tasks without pretending that an agent-generated forecast is public-health guidance.

The v0 problem is not "predict dengue." That framing is too broad and too easy to fake. The sharper problem is:

> Which district-level dengue risk signals are evidence-backed enough to deserve replication and public-health review?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO identifies dengue as a mosquito-borne viral infection and reports that prevention and control rely heavily on vector control and early medical care.
- Verified fact: WHO's 2025 dengue fact sheet identifies climate change, rising temperatures, high rainfall, humidity, urbanization, and health-system limits as factors associated with increased dengue epidemic risk.
- Verified fact: WHO Viet Nam describes dengue infection in Viet Nam as unstable, with annual peaks from June to October, and states that most cases and deaths occur in the central and southern regions.
- Verified fact: A 2023 Nature Communications study analyzed 23 years of district-level dengue case data in Viet Nam and found strong evidence that temperature, infrastructure, hydrometeorology, and mobility interact in dengue emergence.

## Uncertain Areas

- District-level surveillance data access and reporting lag.
- Whether public historical data is granular enough for useful replication.
- Whether rainfall, drought, and heat signals create actionable lead time after accounting for local infrastructure.
- How field teams would use any ranked risk output without distorting local priorities.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Evidence inventory.
- Dataset availability and licensing.
- District-level historical baselines.
- Reproducible exploratory models.
- Red-team review of false positives, false negatives, and harmful operational use.
- Field-reality review from Vietnamese public-health or local implementation contexts.

This pack does not accept direct medical advice, deployment commands, or public outbreak declarations.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated district-level analytic artifact, with clear limits, that a domain reviewer judges suitable for further public-health discussion rather than immediate action.

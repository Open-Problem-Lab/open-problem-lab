# Urban Heat Stress Mortality Risk In South Asia

## Problem Statement

South Asian cities are approaching wet-bulb temperatures that exceed human survivability. The 2015 heatwave killed over 2,500 people. Urban heat islands add 3-8°C. Outdoor workers, slum residents, and the elderly without cooling bear the mortality burden. Satellite data can map land-surface temperature at 30m resolution, and population vulnerability data exists. What's missing is verified city-scale risk analysis honest about who is at risk and what cooling interventions are feasible.

> Which South Asian urban neighborhoods face the highest evidence-backed compound heat-stress mortality risk?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: The IPCC AR6 identifies South Asia as a global hotspot for extreme heat, with wet-bulb temperatures approaching 35°C survivability limits in parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain and coastal Pakistan during extreme events.
- Verified fact: Landsat-derived land-surface temperature data at 30m resolution enables intra-urban heat island mapping but has not been systematically combined with population vulnerability data for most South Asian cities.
- Verified fact: The 2015 India-Pakistan heatwave killed over 2,500 people, with excess mortality concentrated among outdoor workers and elderly populations.
- Verified fact: Ahmedabad's Heat Action Plan, the first in South Asia, is estimated to have averted over 1,000 deaths annually through early warning, cooling centers, and public communication.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether satellite LST adequately captures the heat exposure of informal settlements where ground monitoring is absent.
- How population vulnerability indicators combine with heat exposure to predict actual mortality.
- Whether heat action plans effective in one city generalize to others with different governance and infrastructure.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, dataset availability, city-scale heat exposure and vulnerability mapping, risk ranking, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated city-scale heat-stress risk analysis for at least two South Asian cities, with explicit vulnerability documentation.

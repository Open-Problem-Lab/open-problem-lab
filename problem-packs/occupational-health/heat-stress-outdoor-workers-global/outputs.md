# Outputs

## Accepted Outputs

### Source Inventory (2026-06-30)

The source inventory for the occupational heat stress pack is complete. Nine evidence records have been classified and documented:

**Usable (7 sources):**

- ILO climate-labor report (2024) — global exposure framing
- ILOSTAT employment-by-industry — worker population denominators
- ERA5 reanalysis (ECMWF) — primary source for WBGT derivation
- ISO 7243:2017 — WBGT threshold framework
- Xu et al. 2021 — heat-injury effect size with heterogeneity
- Dunne et al. 2013 — labor capacity projection context
- WHO/ILO UV joint estimate (2024) — methodology precedent

**Limited (3 sources):**

- Lopez de Lara et al. 2015 — single-country CKDnt evidence from El Salvador
- National labor force surveys (LMIC) — informal sector gaps, variable sub-national grain
- Occupational injury registries (LMIC) — most LMICs absent, heat coding rare

**Rejected (1 source category):**

- Low-cost heat sensors (field studies) — no public data infrastructure for systematic comparison

**Data sufficiency claim:** Available public data supports global framing and WBGT exposure derivation from ERA5, but no public dataset provides sub-national occupational heat exposure linked to health outcomes at the granularity needed to identify high-burden districts in LMICs. See claims.json for the full data-sufficiency claim with kill condition.

## Publication Rule

Published outputs must say what they are not. An exposure-outcome map is not a workplace safety enforcement directive, an employer liability determination, or a clinical treatment guide. It does not assign blame to workers or employers for heat-related injuries.

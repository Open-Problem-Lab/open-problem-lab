# Neonatal Mortality And Inpatient Newborn Care Quality

## Problem Statement

2.3 million newborns die each year — half of under-five deaths. Prematurity, birth asphyxia, and sepsis are preventable with quality inpatient care. Yet facility assessments show that even facilities classified as providing newborn care often lack basic resuscitation equipment, thermal care, and infection management. Linking DHS neonatal mortality data to facility-quality assessments at sub-national scale is the missing piece for targeting quality improvement.

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: UNICEF estimates 2.3 million neonatal deaths in 2022, with Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia accounting for over 80 percent, and three causes — prematurity, birth asphyxia, sepsis — responsible for 75 percent.
- Verified fact: Service Provision Assessment surveys document that fewer than 30 percent of facilities in low-income countries classified as providing newborn care have all seven signal functions for inpatient newborn care.
- Verified fact: Kangaroo mother care, resuscitation, and infection management could prevent over 70 percent of neonatal deaths if delivered at scale.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national neonatal care quality gap analysis for at least two low-income countries.

# Pollinator Decline Detection And Crop-Pollination Service Risk In Agricultural Regions

## Problem Statement

The 2016 IPBES global assessment documented that 16 percent of vertebrate pollinators are threatened with extinction and wild pollinator populations are declining across many regions, driven by habitat loss, pesticide exposure, climate change, and invasive species. Crop pollination by insects — predominantly wild and managed bees — contributes an estimated 5-8 percent of global crop production by volume, valued at $235-577 billion annually. Crops that depend on animal pollination include fruits, vegetables, nuts, and oilseeds that are critical for dietary diversity and nutrition.

Yet pollinator monitoring data is almost entirely absent from the regions where pollinator-dependent crop production is growing fastest — Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and Southeast Asia. The IPBES assessment itself noted that over 80 percent of pollinator studies come from Europe and North America. Satellite-derived land-cover data can track habitat change at 10-30m resolution, and MapSPAM provides global crop-production maps at approximately 10km resolution. But linking habitat-loss trends to crop-specific pollination-service risk at sub-national scale has not been done systematically for most LMIC agricultural regions.

> Which agricultural regions in LMICs have the strongest evidence-backed risk of pollinator-decline-driven crop-production loss, based on satellite-derived habitat change and crop-pollination dependence data?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: The IPBES Assessment Report on Pollinators, Pollination and Food Production (2016) found that 16 percent of vertebrate pollinators are threatened with extinction, wild pollinator populations are declining in many regions, and pollinator-dependent crops contribute 5-8 percent of global crop production by volume.
- Verified fact: Pollinator monitoring data is overwhelmingly concentrated in Europe and North America — over 80 percent of studies in the IPBES assessment came from these regions — with almost no systematic wild pollinator population monitoring in Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, or Southeast Asia.
- Verified fact: MapSPAM provides global crop-production maps at approximately 10km resolution distinguishing pollinator-dependent crops (fruits, vegetables, nuts, oilseeds) from wind-pollinated staples (rice, wheat, maize), enabling sub-national estimation of pollination-service dependence.
- Verified fact: Satellite-derived land-cover and land-use change data at 10-30m resolution (Sentinel-2, Landsat) can track habitat fragmentation, agricultural intensification, and pesticide-application proxies, providing indirect indicators of pollinator habitat pressure.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether crop-pollination dependence classifications based on temperate-zone studies accurately reflect pollination ecology in tropical smallholder farming systems, where crop varieties and pollinator communities differ substantially.
- How satellite-derived habitat-change proxies (forest loss, agricultural intensification, landscape simplification) correlate with actual wild pollinator abundance and diversity in the absence of ground monitoring.
- Whether pollination-service risk maps can usefully guide agricultural extension, pesticide regulation, or habitat-conservation decisions in the face of data sparsity.
- How managed honeybee populations interact with wild pollinator populations in LMIC contexts — in some regions, introduced honeybees compete with and displace native pollinators.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Evidence inventory and source-quality assessment for pollinator-monitoring data, crop-pollination dependence classifications, satellite-derived habitat-change data, and pesticide-use proxies.
- Sub-national crop-pollination-dependence mapping from MapSPAM or equivalent crop-production data.
- Habitat-change analysis linking satellite land-cover trends to pollination-service pressure indicators.
- Pollination-service risk mapping at sub-national scale with explicit documentation of proxy-data limitations.
- Red-team review of pollinator-decline overstatement and the risk of eroding farmer confidence in natural pollination services.
- Field-reality review with agricultural research institutions and extension services in key pollinator-dependent crop regions.

This pack does not accept pesticide-ban recommendations, beekeeping-program proposals, or unvalidated crop-loss forecasts attributed to pollinator decline.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated sub-national pollination-service risk analysis for at least two LMIC agricultural regions, with explicit documentation of pollinator-monitoring data gaps, crop-pollination-dependence classification methodology, and habitat-proxy validation limitations.

## Evidence Notes

### IRENA Financing Gap 2024

Use this source for the investment-volume baseline. The 7-fold figure is a modeled projection from SDG7 and climate scenarios — it depends on access assumptions and technology-cost projections. Investment data is country-level aggregate — sub-national distribution is not tracked. The volume gap does not capture the access-structure gap. Always document scenario assumptions and sub-national data limitations.

### Missing Middle Financing Gap

Use this source for the financing-structure barrier. The $50K-$5M range is a general characterization that varies by country context and technology. Perceived-risk documentation is from investor surveys. Equity and grant financing structures are less documented. Always present with country-specific threshold documentation and financing-channel context.

### Concessional Finance Allocation Bias

Use this source for the allocation-disproportionality baseline. Data is country-level aggregate. The disproportionality assessment depends on the comparison metric used — per-capita, per-GDP, or per-access-gap give different conclusions. Concessional definitions vary across institutions. Always document the comparison metric and harmonization methodology.

### National Aggregate Sub-National Gap

Use this source to document the fundamental data constraint: sub-national financing data is almost entirely absent. Investment concentrates in urban and industrial areas while rural last-mile areas receive minimal financing. This is documented qualitatively, not systematically quantified. Always document this data gap explicitly.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

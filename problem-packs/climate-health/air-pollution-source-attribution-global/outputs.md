## Candidate Output Types

- Evidence inventory with source-apportionment methodology comparison and LMIC megacity evidence-gap assessment.
- Sub-national source-attribution map for at least two LMIC megacities with published apportionment studies.
- Sector-specific health-impact analysis applying concentration-response functions to attributed PM2.5.
- Failure-mode review of source-attribution overstatement and borrowed-context risks.
- Field-reality review with air-quality policy and industrial regulation context.

## Publication Rule

Published outputs must say what they are not. A source-attribution map is not an emission inventory, an industrial regulation basis, or a verified source-specific health-impact measurement. Source-attribution percentages depend on methodology — the same city can have different attributed contributions under different methods.

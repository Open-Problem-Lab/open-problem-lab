# Under-Five Mortality Sub-National Inequality In Low-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

Under-five mortality halved globally since 1990 — one of the great development achievements. But the poorest wealth quintile still dies at 2-3x the rate of the richest, and in many countries sub-national inequality has widened even as national averages improved. DHS surveys provide district-level mortality and wealth data, but systematic tracking of whether inequality is growing or shrinking at sub-national scale is not routinely done. Without it, national progress can mask widening local gaps.

> Which sub-national areas show the strongest evidence of widening under-five mortality inequality, and where are intervention-coverage gaps largest among the poorest?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: UNICEF estimates global under-five mortality fell from 93 to 37 per 1,000 live births between 1990 and 2022, but the poorest-richest mortality ratio exceeds 2:1 in over 30 LMICs.
- Verified fact: DHS surveys enable district-level under-five mortality estimation with wealth-quintile disaggregation, providing the data foundation for sub-national inequality tracking.
- Verified fact: A 2020 Lancet Global Health study found that sub-national inequality in child mortality widened in over 40 percent of LMICs between 2000 and 2015 even as national averages declined.
- Verified fact: Intervention coverage — immunization, skilled birth attendance, diarrhea treatment — shows similar wealth gradients, with the poorest quintile receiving 20-50 percent lower coverage than the richest.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether DHS sample sizes are sufficient for wealth-disaggregated district-level inequality estimation. Whether inequality trends can be tracked more frequently than DHS survey intervals. Whether inequality maps change equity-oriented program targeting.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, DHS mortality and wealth data compilation, inequality-trend mapping, intervention-coverage equity analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national under-five mortality inequality trend analysis for at least two low-income countries, with explicit wealth-disaggregation and data-adequacy documentation.

# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. Keep quantitative outputs reproducible.
5. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What evacuation signal claim is being made?
2. What source proves the track, surge, or population threshold relationship?
3. What method produced the verification?
4. What would make this signal kill people instead of save them?
5. Who could misuse a false-alarm statistic to justify reducing warning dissemination?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a forecast verification is methodologically sound. Ask whether a CPP volunteer in a coastal union would change their flag color, megaphone message, or shelter-opening decision based on this output, and what would happen if the signal was wrong. The most dangerous output is one that erodes trust in the one of the few systems proven to save lives at scale.

# Post-Pandemic Learning Loss Measurement And Recovery Tracking In Low-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

The COVID-19 pandemic caused the largest disruption to education in modern history. School closures averaging 20 weeks in low-income countries erased years of learning gains. But the places where learning loss was likely worst — low-income countries with limited pre-pandemic assessment infrastructure — are precisely where the least systematic data exists. Reconstructing what happened from partial data is essential but dangerous when reconstructed estimates are presented as precise measurements.

> Which post-pandemic learning loss estimates in low-income countries are evidence-backed enough to support recovery resource targeting without overstating measurement precision?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: The World Bank and UNESCO estimated that COVID-19 school closures affected 1.6 billion learners in over 190 countries, with closures averaging 20 weeks in low-income countries versus 10 weeks in high-income countries.
- Verified fact: The World Bank's 2022 learning poverty report estimated that the share of children unable to read a simple text by age 10 may have risen from 57 to 70 percent in low- and middle-income countries — an increase of 13 percentage points representing tens of millions of children.
- Verified fact: A 2023 systematic review of learning loss studies found that losses of 0.1-0.4 standard deviations in math and reading were common, but studies were concentrated in high-income countries with good pre-pandemic assessment data, making global extrapolation uncertain.
- Verified fact: Several low-income countries conduct Early Grade Reading Assessments or similar, but these are irregular, vary by donor program, and lack consistent longitudinal tracking.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether irregular assessment data across different years, instruments, and languages can be combined into valid learning loss estimates.
- How school-closure duration interacts with household wealth, parental education, and remote-learning access to produce heterogeneous learning loss.
- Whether recovery is occurring, and at what rate, in countries without longitudinal assessment systems.
- How to communicate learning loss estimates without creating defeatism about the affected cohort.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Evidence inventory and source quality assessment.
- Dataset availability including cross-country assessment data, country-specific learning assessments, and school-closure data.
- Learning loss estimation methodology with explicit uncertainty communication.
- Recovery tracking from post-pandemic assessment rounds.
- Red-team review of over-precise or defeatist communication.
- Field-reality review with education ministry decision context.

This pack does not accept education budget recommendations, curriculum mandates, or unvalidated learning loss forecasts.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated learning loss estimate for at least two low-income countries, with explicit data-quality tiers and uncertainty bounds, that a domain reviewer judges suitable for recovery discussion rather than policy allocation.

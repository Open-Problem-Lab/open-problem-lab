# Validation

## Validation Layers

| Layer           | Gate                                                                 |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Structure       | `pnpm validate` passes schemas and problem-pack completeness checks  |
| Evidence        | Every claim has a dated evidence record                              |
| Reproducibility | Quantitative artifacts include rerunnable steps                      |
| Review          | Required reviewer roles approve or request changes                   |
| Replication     | Safety-sensitive or quantitative claims are independently reproduced |

## Baseline Requirements

Sub-national grassland degradation analyses must document:

- Satellite vegetation product used (MODIS NDVI, Rangeland Analysis Platform) with resolution and temporal coverage.
- Climate normalization methodology for distinguishing drought-driven variability from persistent degradation.
- Spatial resolution limitations relative to pastoralist decision-making scales.
- Country-level data availability assessment — explicitly document Mongolia vs. other Central Asian countries.
- Administrative boundary version and sub-national aggregation method.

## NDVI-Trend Requirements

No grassland degradation signal may be called persistent until it includes:

- Multi-decade trend analysis (minimum 15 years) with statistical significance testing.
- Climate normalization using precipitation or soil-moisture data to filter drought-year effects.
- Comparison with at least one published NDVI-trend study for a similar rangeland system.
- Sensitivity analysis showing how degradation classification changes under alternative trend methods or time windows.
- Explicit documentation that NDVI trends are productivity indicators, not direct measures of forage availability or livestock carrying capacity.

## Livelihood-Correlation Requirements

No pastoral livelihood claim linked to grassland degradation may be merged without:

- Direct livelihood data source documentation with grain, frequency, and coverage assessment.
- Data-sparsity documentation stating what systematic monitoring would be needed to validate the correlation.
- Mongolia-specific vs. broader Central Asian evidence distinction — do not extrapolate Mongolia findings without explicit transferability caveats.
- Confound controls for non-degradation livelihood drivers (market access, policy changes, dzud events).
- Non-use case documentation (not for grazing-access restriction, not for livestock insurance claim denial, not for pastoral resettlement justification).

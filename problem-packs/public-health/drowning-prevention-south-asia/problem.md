# Child Drowning Mortality And Supervision Gaps In South Asia

## Problem Statement

Drowning kills 230,000 people annually. Children under five have the highest rates, and over 90 percent of deaths occur in LMICs. South Asia has the highest absolute numbers — Bangladesh alone records 14,000 child drowning deaths per year. Most deaths occur within 20 meters of home, in ponds and ditches that are ubiquitous in the rural landscape. Mapping water-body proximity against childcare availability at sub-national scale is the missing prevention tool.

> Which sub-national areas in South Asia have the highest evidence-backed child drowning mortality risk relative to childcare supervision access?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO estimates 236,000 drowning deaths globally in 2019, with children under five at highest risk and LMICs accounting for over 90 percent of deaths.
- Verified fact: The Bangladesh Health and Injury Survey found drowning is the leading cause of death for children aged 1-4, with over 14,000 annual deaths, the majority occurring in ponds within 20 meters of the home.
- Verified fact: Anchal (community creche) programs in Bangladesh have been shown to reduce child drowning by over 80 percent through supervised childcare during peak-risk hours.
- Verified fact: OpenStreetMap and satellite-derived water-body maps at 10m resolution exist for most of South Asia but have not been linked to drowning and childcare-access data.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether satellite-derived water-body maps capture small ponds and ditches that pose the highest risk to toddlers. Whether childcare-access data is sufficient for sub-national risk mapping. Whether risk maps change creche-placement or drowning-prevention strategy.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, water-body mapping, drowning-mortality compilation, childcare-access analysis, risk mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national drowning risk analysis for at least two South Asian countries.

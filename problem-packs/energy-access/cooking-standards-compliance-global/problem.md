# Clean Cooking Standards Compliance And Stove Performance Verification Gaps In Low-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

Household air pollution from solid-fuel cooking kills an estimated 3.2 million people annually — more than malaria, tuberculosis, and HIV/AIDS combined. The clean-cooking sector has responded with improved cookstoves, ethanol and biogas stoves, and LPG systems, supported by ISO 19867-1 and IWA performance standards. But standards compliance verification is almost entirely absent for the hundreds of millions of stoves deployed in LMICs. Laboratory performance tests show 50-90 percent emission reductions, yet field studies consistently find that real-world performance degrades by 30-70 percent due to fuel moisture, cooking practices, stove maintenance, and the gap between controlled testing and household use.

The compliance-verification gap is the core problem: stove manufacturers and programs can claim standards compliance based on laboratory tests, but field performance — the performance that actually determines health impact — is rarely measured. Without verified field-performance data, clean-cooking investments cannot distinguish stoves that deliver real emission reductions from stoves that perform well only in the lab.

> Which clean-cooking stove categories and deployment contexts have the strongest evidence-backed gap between laboratory standards compliance and verified field performance?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: ISO 19867-1:2018 and IWA clean-cooking standards define performance tiers for thermal efficiency, emissions, and safety, but compliance verification through independent testing is absent for most stoves sold and deployed in LMICs.
- Verified fact: Field studies consistently find 30-70 percent degradation in stove performance relative to laboratory results, driven by fuel moisture, cooking practices, stove maintenance, and the gap between controlled testing and household use.
- Verified fact: Household air pollution from solid-fuel cooking causes an estimated 3.2 million premature deaths annually, with the burden concentrated in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia where clean-cooking adoption rates remain below 20 percent.
- Verified fact: The Global Alliance for Clean Cookstoves and national programs have deployed hundreds of millions of improved stoves, but systematic field-performance monitoring covers fewer than 5 percent of deployed stoves in most countries.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether laboratory performance tiers predict field performance in specific cooking-fuel and cooking-practice contexts.
- How stove maintenance and user behavior interact with stove technology in determining actual emission reductions.
- Whether compliance-verification data changes purchasing decisions, program funding, or manufacturer behavior.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Evidence inventory and source-quality assessment for ISO/IWA standards, laboratory test data, field-performance studies, and compliance-verification systems.
- Laboratory-to-field performance gap analysis for major stove categories.
- Compliance-verification system assessment across LMIC countries.
- Red-team review of laboratory-performance overstatement and the risk of health-impact claims based on lab results rather than field data.
- Field-reality review with clean-cooking programs, stove manufacturers, and health ministries.

This pack does not accept stove-design recommendations, fuel-subsidy proposals, or unvalidated health-impact claims based on laboratory performance data.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated laboratory-to-field performance gap analysis for at least two stove categories and two LMIC contexts, with explicit documentation of field-performance measurement methodology, compliance-verification gaps, and the distinction between laboratory and verified field performance.

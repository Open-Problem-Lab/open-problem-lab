# Mangrove Deforestation And Coastal Protection Loss In Southeast Asia

## Problem Statement

Southeast Asia has lost more than 30 percent of its mangrove forests since 1980 — over 100,000 square kilometers cleared primarily for shrimp aquaculture, rice cultivation, and coastal development. Indonesia alone accounts for roughly one-third of global mangrove loss. Mangroves reduce storm-surge height by 30-70 percent through wave-energy dissipation, protect shorelines from erosion, sequester carbon at rates 3-5 times higher than terrestrial forests, and serve as nursery habitat for commercially important fish and shrimp species. The 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami demonstrated that coastal communities behind intact mangroves experienced substantially lower mortality than those behind cleared shorelines.

Global Mangrove Watch now provides annual mangrove-extent maps at 25-meter resolution derived from Landsat and Sentinel imagery, covering 1996 to present. Menendez et al. (2020, Scientific Reports) valued global mangrove flood-protection benefits at $65 billion annually. But these global estimates have not been translated into sub-national analyses that quantify how much coastal protection specific communities have lost from specific deforestation events — and where the remaining mangroves face the highest conversion pressure.

> Which coastal areas in Southeast Asia have lost the most mangrove-based storm-surge protection service, and where are the remaining intact mangroves most threatened by near-term conversion pressure?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: Global Mangrove Watch, using Landsat and Sentinel-2 imagery at 25m resolution, estimates Southeast Asia lost over 30 percent of mangrove cover since 1980. Indonesia, Myanmar, and Thailand account for the largest absolute losses, with shrimp-aquaculture expansion as the primary driver.
- Verified fact: Mangroves reduce storm-surge height by 30-70 percent depending on mangrove width, tree density, species composition, and bathymetry. Wave-energy attenuation of 50-90 percent has been documented within the first 100-500 meters of mangrove forest.
- Verified fact: A 2020 Scientific Reports study by Menendez et al. valued global mangrove flood-protection services at $65 billion annually in avoided damages, with Southeast Asia accounting for the largest share of protection value due to high coastal population density and cyclone frequency.
- Verified fact: Mangrove deforestation rates have slowed in Thailand and Vietnam due to restoration programs, but accelerated in Indonesia and Myanmar, with annual loss rates of 0.2-0.5 percent of remaining area per year in the highest-pressure regions.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether 25-meter-resolution mangrove maps adequately capture narrow fringing mangroves and small patches that nonetheless provide substantial local wave attenuation.
- How bathymetric data quality affects the translation of mangrove-width measurements into storm-surge attenuation estimates at local scale.
- Whether mangrove-protection valuation influences actual land-use decisions given the powerful economic incentives driving aquaculture expansion.
- How mangrove-restoration potential interacts with sea-level rise — restored mangroves need sediment accretion to keep pace with rising seas.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Evidence inventory and source-quality assessment for mangrove-extent data, storm-surge models, coastal population data, and aquaculture-expansion drivers.
- Sub-national mangrove-change analysis linking deforestation events to lost coastal-protection service.
- Coastal-protection-loss valuation at sub-national scale using population, infrastructure, and storm-frequency data.
- Remaining-mangrove threat analysis identifying areas of highest near-term conversion pressure.
- Red-team review of protection-valuation overstatement and displacement risks for coastal communities.
- Field-reality review with coastal-zone management authorities and mangrove-restoration practitioners.

This pack does not accept aquaculture-policy recommendations, coastal-development permitting advice, or unvalidated mangrove-carbon-credit claims.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated sub-national mangrove-loss coastal-protection analysis for at least two Southeast Asian countries, with explicit deforestation-driver documentation, storm-surge-model uncertainty bounds, and remaining-mangrove threat assessment.

## Required Dataset Properties

- Date range and temporal resolution.
- Geographic grain and coordinate reference system.
- Elevation datum and vertical accuracy.
- SLR projection scenario and model ensemble.
- Population estimate year and methodology.
- License or reuse permission.

## Rejection Rule

A dataset is rejected for canonical modeling if grain, vertical accuracy, date range, license, or method cannot be verified. Rejected datasets may still be listed as context.

## Key Gap: SIDS-Scale DEM and Bathymetry

The single most consequential data gap for SIDS coastal exposure analysis is the absence of high-resolution, high-vertical-accuracy coastal elevation models for atoll nations. CoastalDEM at 90 m with 0.5 m residual error is the best global product, but its error is comparable to the SLR signal being mapped. Lidar coverage exists for some individual SIDS (e.g., parts of Maldives and Marshall Islands) but is not publicly available as a unified dataset. Any island-scale exposure map built on CoastalDEM must carry vertical-accuracy uncertainty bounds that may span the difference between minimal and catastrophic inundation.

# Postharvest Food Loss In Sub-Saharan Africa

## Problem Statement

FAO estimates 30-40 percent of Sub-Saharan African food is lost postharvest — enough to feed 300 million people. Losses are highest at farm-to-market stages where storage, cold chains, and processing are weakest. APHLIS provides postharvest loss estimates for cereals at sub-national scale. But linking crop-production maps, storage-infrastructure data, and household-survey food-loss modules to identify where losses are highest and most reducible is underdeveloped.

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: FAO estimates 30-40 percent of food produced in Sub-Saharan Africa is lost postharvest, concentrated at handling, storage, and transport stages.
- Verified fact: APHLIS provides postharvest loss estimates for maize, sorghum, millet, and other cereals at provincial scale across SSA, with losses ranging from 10-30 percent by crop and location.
- Verified fact: Hermetic storage bags and metal silos can reduce on-farm storage losses by 50-80 percent, but adoption is below 10 percent in most SSA countries.
- Verified fact: Cold-chain infrastructure for perishable crops (fruits, vegetables, dairy) is almost absent outside major urban corridors.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether APHLIS estimates can be validated with household-level food-loss measurement. Whether storage-infrastructure maps predict loss levels.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national postharvest food loss analysis for at least two countries.

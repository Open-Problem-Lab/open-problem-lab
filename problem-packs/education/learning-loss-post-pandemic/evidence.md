## Evidence Notes

### World Bank Learning Poverty 2022

Use this source as the most widely cited learning loss framing. The 57-to-70 percent increase is a modeled simulation, not a direct measurement — treat it as a directional estimate, not a precise figure. The methodology depends heavily on school-closure duration assumptions.

### UNESCO School Closures

Use this source for school-closure duration data. The 20-week vs 10-week low-income vs high-income gap is well-documented and explains differential learning loss. Partial closure reporting is inconsistent across countries.

### Betthäuser et al. 2023 Review

Use this source to establish that learning loss evidence is concentrated in wealthy countries. The 85 percent high-income-country concentration is the key finding — not the 0.14 SD average, which may not generalize.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

### Agricultural Value Chain Electrification

Use this source for the strongest evidence on value-chain-specific productive use. Equipment adoption depends on affordability, technical capacity, and market access — electricity alone does not guarantee productive use. Most studies measure ownership rather than actual usage frequency. Publication bias toward positive findings cannot be excluded. Always present with value-chain specificity and adoption-barrier documentation.

### Mini-Grid Productive Use Demand

Use this source (ESMAP Mini Grids market outlook) to document that residential connections dominate Sub-Saharan African mini-grid demand and productive-use growth often lags projections. ### Enterprise Formation Electrification

Use this source for the business-formation effect. The 5-15 percent increase is pooled across diverse contexts — isolated communities without market access may see near-zero effects. Enterprise surveys miss informal productive use. Follow-up periods are 2-5 years — long-term sustainability is rarely assessed. Always present with context-dependency documentation.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

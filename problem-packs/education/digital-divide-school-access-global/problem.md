# Digital Divide Measurement And School Internet Connectivity In Low-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

UNICEF and ITU estimate 1.3 billion school-age children — two-thirds globally — have no internet at home. Two-thirds of schools in low-income countries lack any connection. The digital divide is not just about devices; it is about the connectivity infrastructure that enables digital learning. Yet school-level connectivity data is fragmented across countries and rarely updated. Measuring the divide is the prerequisite for closing it.

> Which sub-national school connectivity gaps are evidence-backed enough to support infrastructure investment targeting?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: UNICEF and ITU estimate 1.3 billion school-age children lack home internet access, and two-thirds of schools in low-income countries lack any internet connection.
- Verified fact: Giga, a UNICEF-ITU initiative, has mapped school locations and connectivity status for over 1 million schools across 30 countries, using satellite imagery and machine learning to identify school locations and connectivity.
- Verified fact: School census data in many low-income countries includes ICT indicators — computer availability, internet connectivity, electricity — but data currency varies widely and is not systematically cross-validated.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether satellite-derived connectivity classifications match ground-truthed school reports, and whether connectivity maps translate into actual infrastructure investment.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, school connectivity data compilation, connectivity gap mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national school connectivity gap analysis for at least two low-income countries.

# Conflict-Driven Health Service Disruption In Fragile States

## Problem Statement

Over 2 billion people live in conflict-affected countries where health systems are under active attack. WHO's Surveillance System for Attacks on Healthcare documented over 1,500 attacks in 2023 alone. But beyond counting attacks, measuring which facilities stop functioning, which services are disrupted, and how many patients lose access is the humanitarian priority. ACLED conflict data and health-facility registries exist — linking them to verified service-disruption signals is the gap.

> Which health facilities and services in conflict-affected areas show the strongest evidence-backed disruption signals, and which populations lose access?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO documented over 1,500 attacks on healthcare in 2023 across conflict zones, with the majority in Ukraine, Palestine, Sudan, and Myanmar. Facility-level service disruption data remains fragmentary.
- Verified fact: ACLED conflict-event data provides geolocated, dated records of armed conflict events including attacks on health infrastructure, enabling proximity-based disruption estimation.
- Verified fact: DHIS2 health management information systems in several conflict-affected countries continue reporting even during active conflict, providing a service-utilization signal that can be compared against pre-conflict baselines.
- Verified fact: A 2022 Lancet study found that conflict-zone facilities within 25 km of armed clashes experienced 30-60 percent reductions in outpatient visits and 40-80 percent reductions in maternal health services.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether DHIS2 reporting completeness during conflict is sufficient for disruption estimation. Whether proximity-based disruption proxies match facility-reported disruption. Whether disruption maps change humanitarian-health response decisions.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, conflict-event data compilation, facility-service-utilization analysis, disruption mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated conflict-health-disruption analysis for at least two fragile states.

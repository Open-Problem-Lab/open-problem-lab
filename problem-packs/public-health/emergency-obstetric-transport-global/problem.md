# Emergency Obstetric Transport Access In Low-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

The three-delays model identifies reaching a facility as the second most lethal gap in maternal survival. Yet ambulance coverage data is absent from sub-national health information in most low-income countries. OpenStreetMap road networks and MAP travel-time surfaces exist globally — linking these to ambulance-depot locations and maternal mortality data at district scale is the missing operational analysis for emergency obstetric transport planning.

> Which sub-national areas have the highest evidence-backed gap between maternal mortality risk and emergency obstetric transport access?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO estimates that delay in reaching a facility contributes to 20-30 percent of maternal deaths in low-income countries, with travel times exceeding 2 hours for emergency obstetric care in over 40 percent of rural districts.
- Verified fact: OpenStreetMap road-network data and MAP travel-time surfaces at 1km resolution exist globally, enabling population-level emergency-transport access estimation.
- Verified fact: Ambulance-registry data is fragmented — fewer than 20 low-income countries maintain geo-located ambulance-depot databases, and emergency-medical-services dispatch systems are absent from most rural districts.
- Verified fact: Community-based emergency transport schemes using motorcycle ambulances and volunteer drivers have demonstrated feasibility in multiple Sub-Saharan African countries.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether OSM road-network data captures seasonal road conditions. Whether ambulance-depot data can be crowdsourced or inferred from health-facility registries. Whether transport-gap maps change EMS investment or ambulance deployment.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, facility-location compilation, road-network and travel-time analysis, ambulance-coverage mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national emergency obstetric transport gap analysis for at least two low-income countries.

# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. For LST analysis: always document that LST measures surface radiative temperature, not air temperature — the transfer function varies by climate zone, humidity, and urban geometry. Validate against ground stations where available and document where validation is absent.
5. For green-space classification: always document green-space types (parks, street trees, green roofs, community gardens) and irrigation status — unirrigated vegetation in water-scarce cities provides minimal cooling during heat waves.
6. For vulnerability analysis: always document informal-settlement green-space deficits relative to formal areas and the risk that green-space improvement triggers gentrification.
7. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made about urban green-space cooling or heat-mortality reduction?
2. What source proves it — and is it from a temperate high-income city study or an LMIC tropical context?
3. What method translates satellite LST to the air temperature that drives heat-stress physiology, and what is the validation status?
4. What would make it false — particularly if the LST-to-air-temperature transfer doesn't hold in tropical humidity, if cooling depends on irrigation that is unavailable, or if green-space improvement displaces the vulnerable populations it aims to protect?
5. Who could misuse it — particularly to justify green-gentrification, to promise cooling that irrigation constraints cannot deliver, or to transfer temperate-city cooling estimates to tropical LMIC contexts?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a cooling-effect map is impressive. Ask whether it would change a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For urban green-space cooling, ask specifically:

- Would a Nairobi City County urban planning director use this to target green-space investment in informal settlements, or would the investment trigger gentrification that displaces the very populations it aims to protect?
- Would a municipal tree-planting program use this to select species and locations, or would it promise cooling that water-scarcity constraints cannot deliver?
- Would an informal-settlement upgrading program use this to justify green-space allocation, or would it ignore the land-tenure and displacement risks?

The answer must include explicit non-use cases before any field-facing output is published.

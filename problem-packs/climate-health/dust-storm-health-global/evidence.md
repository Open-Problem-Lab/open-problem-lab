## Evidence Notes

### Goudie 2014 Dust Health Review

Use this source for the dust-health impact baseline with the critical caveat that the 5-10 percent hospitalization increase is pooled from studies concentrated in high-income arid countries (US Southwest, Australia, Israel). LMIC arid regions are severely underrepresented. The review does not distinguish health effects by dust-source mineralogy or biological contaminant content. Confound control for non-dust PM10 varies across studies. Do not apply pooled estimates to specific LMIC arid regions without documenting the evidence gap.

### Satellite Dust Detection

Use MODIS AOD and CAMS dust forecasts for dust-exposure mapping with the critical caveat that AOD retrieval accuracy is reduced over bright desert surfaces. AOD measures column-integrated aerosol, not ground-level concentration — the transfer to ground PM10 depends on boundary-layer height, dust altitude, and deposition rates. CAMS at 40 km resolution smooths local variability. Always document these limitations in exposure analyses.

### Dust Biological Contaminants

Use this source to document that dust PM10 may be more harmful per unit mass than non-dust PM10 due to biological and chemical contaminants. The differential toxicity finding is not yet consensus — it is based on limited studies. Contaminant profiles vary enormously by source region. Health studies rarely measure contaminant-specific exposure. Always present differential-toxicity claims with explicit uncertainty.

### Ground Monitor Sparsity

Use this source to document the fundamental evidence constraint: most LMIC arid countries have fewer than one PM10 monitor per million people. This means satellite exposure estimates cannot be validated at sub-national scale and health-impact attribution is uncertain. Document this sparsity explicitly in every output.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

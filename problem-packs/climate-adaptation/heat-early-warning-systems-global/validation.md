# Validation

## Validation Layers

| Layer           | Gate                                                                 |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Structure       | `pnpm validate` passes schemas and problem-pack completeness checks  |
| Evidence        | Every claim has a dated evidence record                              |
| Reproducibility | Quantitative artifacts include rerunnable steps                      |
| Review          | Required reviewer roles approve or request changes                   |
| Replication     | Safety-sensitive or quantitative claims are independently reproduced |

## Baseline Requirements

Sub-national heat early warning analyses must document:

- Warning-system data source (WMO, IFRC, national meteorological agency) with date, geographic scope, and operational status classification.
- Mortality-data quality assessment — vital registration completeness, heat-specific cause-of-death coding availability.
- Alternative mortality data sources where vital registration is inadequate (sentinel surveillance, burial records, all-cause mortality statistical methods).
- Climate-trend adjustment methodology for shifting heat-wave baselines.
- The distinction between system existence and verified operational capacity at sub-national scale.

## Effectiveness-Evaluation Requirements

No mortality-reduction effectiveness claim may be called actionable until it includes:

- Mortality-data source and quality assessment for the analyzed LMIC context.
- Climate-trend adjustment methodology that accounts for increasing heat-wave frequency and intensity.
- Confound controls for secular trends in healthcare access, housing quality, and air conditioning adoption.
- Comparison with at least one published effectiveness study, with explicit LMIC-transferability assessment.
- Existence-vs-effectiveness distinction — document that system listing does not equal verified protection.
- Sensitivity analysis showing how effectiveness estimates change under alternative mortality-data sources or confound specifications.

## Coverage-Mapping Requirements

No warning-system coverage claim may imply protection without:

- Operational status documentation — confirm the system is actually issuing warnings, not just listed as existing.
- Population-reach assessment — estimate what proportion of vulnerable populations actually receive warnings.
- Response-capacity documentation — assess whether cooling centers, water, and emergency services are available when warnings are issued.
- Non-use case documentation (not for claiming heat mortality is solved, not for justifying underinvestment in other adaptation measures).

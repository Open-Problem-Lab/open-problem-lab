# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. For NDVI trend analysis: always document climate normalization methodology, the distinction between drought-driven variability and persistent degradation, and spatial resolution limitations relative to pastoralist decision-making scales.
5. For livelihood correlation: always document data-sparsity, the Mongolia-vs-other-countries evidence distinction, and that livestock census and household survey data are irregular across Central Asia.
6. For degradation classification: always present results as productivity-trend indicators, not as verified irreversible degradation without climate disambiguation.
7. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made about grassland degradation or pastoral livelihood impact?
2. What source proves it — and is it satellite NDVI, livestock census, household survey, or assumption?
3. What method distinguishes climate-driven variability from persistent degradation?
4. What would make it false — particularly if the NDVI decline reflects drought recovery potential, if livestock data is too sparse for correlation, or if Mongolia findings don't transfer to other Central Asian countries?
5. Who could misuse it — particularly to restrict pastoralist grazing access, justify livestock insurance claim denials, or overstate pastoral welfare decline?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a degradation map is impressive. Ask whether it would change a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For Central Asian grasslands, ask specifically:

- Would a Mongolia Ministry of Food and Agriculture livestock officer use this to pre-position dzud emergency resources, or would it be treated as verified degradation when it's actually a drought-year NDVI decline?
- Would a livestock insurance program use this to calibrate index-insurance triggers, or would it deny claims based on satellite signals that don't reflect actual forage availability?
- Would a rangeland management agency use this to plan rotational grazing, or would it restrict pastoralist access to areas classified as degraded without ground verification?

The answer must include explicit non-use cases before any field-facing output is published.

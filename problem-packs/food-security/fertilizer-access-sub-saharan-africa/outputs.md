## Candidate Output Types

- Evidence inventory with fertilizer-use data granularity and soil-nutrient data coverage assessment.
- Sub-national fertilizer-access gap map for at least two SSA countries with distribution-infrastructure documentation.
- Soil nutrient depletion and fertilizer-response analysis with response-variability documentation.
- Failure-mode review of fertilizer-access overstatement and blanket-recommendation risks.
- Field-reality review with agricultural ministry and soil-health program context.

## Publication Rule

Published outputs must say what they are not. A fertilizer-access gap map is not a fertilizer-subsidy recommendation — soil-specific response variability means blanket subsidies without soil analysis may waste resources. Soil nutrient depletion data from research sites may not represent all farming systems. National averages mask enormous sub-national variation.

# Drinking Water Quality Monitoring Gaps In LMICs

## Problem Statement

2 billion people drink fecally contaminated water. Yet fewer than 30 countries report sub-national water-quality data to JMP. Most LMICs test water quality sporadically if at all. Water-point mapping and satellite-derived environmental risk factors can estimate contamination risk, but linking these to systematic monitoring-gap identification is the missing operational tool.

> Which sub-national areas in LMICs have the largest evidence-backed gap between drinking water quality monitoring coverage and fecal contamination risk?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO/UNICEF JMP estimates 2 billion people drink water contaminated with feces. Fewer than 30 countries report sub-national E. coli or thermotolerant coliform data.
- Verified fact: Water-point mapping projects (WPDx, Akvo, mWater) have mapped millions of water points globally with functionality and water-quality test data.
- Verified fact: Satellite-derived environmental risk factors — proximity to open defecation, flood risk, livestock density — can proxy for contamination risk where testing is absent.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, JMP data compilation, monitoring-gap mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national water-quality monitoring gap analysis for at least two LMICs.

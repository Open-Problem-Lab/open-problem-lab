# Productive Use Of Energy For Agricultural Value Chains In Rural Areas

## Problem Statement

Electrification alone does not generate economic development — it is the productive use of electricity in income-generating activities that drives rural prosperity. Yet the link between electricity access and productive use is weak in most rural contexts. World Bank estimates that electrification for productive agricultural use can increase rural incomes by 20-30 percent, but fewer than 10 percent of mini-grids in Sub-Saharan Africa serve productive-use customers. The gap between electricity availability and verified productive use is the core measurement problem.

Productive-use data comes from mini-grid operator surveys, enterprise surveys, and agricultural value-chain studies, but coverage is sparse and fragmented. Most mini-grid operators track connection counts, not productive-use applications. Agricultural value-chain studies document where electricity could enable processing, cold storage, and irrigation, but rarely track whether electrification actually leads to adoption of productive-use equipment. Without verified productive-use data, energy-access investments cannot be targeted where they would most increase rural incomes.

> Which rural areas in LMICs have the strongest evidence-backed gap between electricity access and productive use in agricultural value chains?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: World Bank estimates that electrification for productive use in agriculture can increase rural incomes by 20-30 percent, but fewer than 10 percent of mini-grids in Sub-Saharan Africa serve productive-use customers — the gap between access and productive use is the core challenge.
- Verified fact: Agricultural value-chain applications — milling, drying, cold storage, irrigation, and processing — represent the highest-impact productive-use opportunities in rural areas, but equipment adoption depends on affordability, technical capacity, and market access that electricity alone does not guarantee.
- Verified fact: Mini-grid operators in SSA report that residential lighting connections dominate their customer base, with productive-use loads typically representing less than 20 percent of total demand — the business model for rural mini-grids depends on productive-use growth that rarely materializes at projected rates.
- Verified fact: Enterprise surveys in rural electrification areas show that electricity access increases business formation by 5-15 percent on average, but the effect is highly context-dependent — areas with existing market infrastructure and human capital benefit more than isolated rural communities.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether the 20-30 percent income increase from productive-use electrification generalizes across agricultural contexts, or whether effects are concentrated in specific value chains and market-connected areas.
- How equipment affordability, technical maintenance capacity, and market access interact with electricity availability in determining productive-use adoption.
- Whether productive-use gap maps change mini-grid investment targeting, agricultural-extension program design, or equipment-subsidy decisions.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Evidence inventory and source-quality assessment for mini-grid operator data, enterprise surveys, agricultural value-chain studies, and productive-use evaluations.
- Sub-national productive-use gap mapping using mini-grid customer data and agricultural value-chain analysis.
- Income-impact analysis linking electrification to agricultural productivity and rural income with appropriate confound controls.
- Red-team review of productive-use overstatement and the risk that electrification without complementary support fails to generate projected income gains.
- Field-reality review with mini-grid operators, agricultural extension services, and rural development programs.

This pack does not accept mini-grid design recommendations, equipment-subsidy proposals, or unvalidated income-gain claims based on projected rather than observed productive use.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated sub-national productive-use gap analysis for at least two LMIC contexts, with explicit documentation of mini-grid customer data limitations, agricultural value-chain specificity, and the distinction between electricity access and verified productive use.

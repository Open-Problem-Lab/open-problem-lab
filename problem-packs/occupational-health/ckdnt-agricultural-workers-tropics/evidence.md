## Evidence Notes

### Correa-Rotter et al. Mesoamerican Nephropathy Review (2019)

Use this source for the current authoritative review of Mesoamerican nephropathy epidemiology, case definition, and the state of etiology evidence. The review classifies CKDnt as a distinct disease entity and presents both heat-dehydration and agrochemical hypotheses without resolving causation.

### Gonzalez-Quiroz et al. Kidney Decline in Young Adults (2018)

Use this source for prospective cohort evidence of kidney function decline in at-risk young adults. The study provides longitudinal data but over two years and without individual-level agrochemical exposure measurement. It supports the heat hypothesis but does not rule out the agrochemical hypothesis.

### Wesseling et al. Heat Stress as Primary Driver (2020)

Use this source for the strongest published argument that heat stress is the primary etiological driver. Note that this is an argument-driven review, not a systematic review, and the author group has primarily studied the heat-dehydration pathway, which introduces potential confirmation bias. The title states a causal position that the evidence does not fully establish.

### Jayasumana et al. Glyphosate and Fluoride in Water (2023)

Use this source for ecological-level evidence supporting the agrochemical-water quality hypothesis in Sri Lanka. The study found associations between water contaminants and CKDu but cannot demonstrate individual-level nephrotoxic dose. Two published comments in the same journal question the analytical methods. Treat as supporting but not conclusive evidence for the agrochemical hypothesis.

### Haas et al. Pathology of Mesoamerican Nephropathy (2018)

Use this source for the pathological evidence that CKDnt is a distinct disease entity with a characteristic pattern of chronic tubulointerstitial injury and glomerulosclerosis. This source supports disease classification but does not determine etiology.

### Environmental Health Pesticide-CKDu Review (2017)

Use this source for the systematic review of pesticide exposure and CKDu. The review found associations but identified critical limitations including ecological exposure assessment and inability to separate pesticide exposure from heat stress. This is the strongest synthesis of the agrochemical hypothesis evidence base.

### Garcia-Trabanino et al. Dialysis Enrollment Guatemala (2015)

Use this source for evidence of the disease burden and its concentration in agricultural communities. Dialysis enrollment reflects treatment access, not disease prevalence, so this is a lower-bound burden indicator. Use for scale arguments, not for causal inference.

### Osborne et al. Environmental Factors Review (2026)

Use this source for the most recent narrative synthesis of environmental factors across all affected regions. The review concludes that multiple exposures likely contribute but that disentangling individual causal pathways remains the central challenge.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack. For CKDnt, the additional requirement is that each evidence record must state which etiology hypothesis it addresses and at what level of causal inference.

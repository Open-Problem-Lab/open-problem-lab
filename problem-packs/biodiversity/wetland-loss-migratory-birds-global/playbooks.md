# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. For wetland-change analysis: always document satellite resolution limitations for small ephemeral wetlands, tidal-cycle complications for intertidal sites, and cloud-cover temporal gaps.
5. For bird-population correlation: always document IWC and eBird monitoring-coverage gaps, temporal consistency limitations, and spatial bias toward accessible sites.
6. For flyway-scale attribution: always present wetland loss as one of multiple threats — document hunting, pollution, climate change, and disturbance as co-occurring drivers.
7. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made about wetland loss and migratory bird population decline?
2. What source proves it — and is it satellite wetland-change data, bird-population counts, or a correlation between the two?
3. What method separates wetland-loss effects from other flyway threats (hunting, pollution, climate, disturbance)?
4. What would make it false — particularly if the satellite misses small wetlands, if bird monitoring is too sparse at critical sites, or if multi-threat attribution is incomplete?
5. Who could misuse it — particularly to justify conservation-priority changes without multi-threat context, or to over-extrapolate site-level findings to flyway scale?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a wetland-bird correlation is impressive. Ask whether it would change a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For migratory flyways, ask specifically:

- Would an EAAFP secretariat officer use this to prioritize stopover-site conservation funding, or would it divert attention from non-wetland threats to declining species?
- Would a Ramsar Convention body use this to trigger site-protection action, or would it over-extrapolate site-level findings to flyway-scale policy?
- Would a national wildlife agency use this to justify coastal reclamation moratoriums, or would it ignore the economic context of wetland conversion?

The answer must include explicit non-use cases before any field-facing output is published.

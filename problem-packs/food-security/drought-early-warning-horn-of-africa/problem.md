# Drought Early Warning And Food Insecurity Prediction In The Horn Of Africa

## Problem Statement

The Horn of Africa's 2020-2023 drought was the worst in 40 years, pushing 23 million people into acute food insecurity. FEWS NET and IPC classify food insecurity phases, but the signals that predict phase deterioration — rainfall deficits, vegetation stress, livestock conditions, market prices — have lead times of unclear reliability. The question is which signals at which lead times are verified enough to trigger humanitarian action without false alarms that waste scarce resources.

> Which rainfall, vegetation, and market-price signals at which lead times are evidence-backed enough to improve food insecurity early warning in the Horn of Africa?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: FEWS NET and IPC estimated over 23 million people in Ethiopia, Somalia, and Kenya faced Crisis or worse food insecurity during the 2020-2023 drought, with Somalia at risk of famine in 2022.
- Verified fact: CHIRPS rainfall data, MODIS NDVI, and market price monitoring provide early warning inputs, but their lead-time reliability for predicting IPC phase deterioration has not been systematically verified.
- Verified fact: A 2021 systematic review found that rainfall-based early warning systems predict food insecurity with moderate skill at 1-3 month leads but degrade rapidly beyond 6 months and fail to capture conflict-driven food crises.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether rainfall deficits alone can distinguish between moderate and severe food insecurity without market-price and conflict data.
- How livestock body-condition data could improve lead time over vegetation-only signals.
- Whether early action triggered by forecast-based financing mechanisms reduces food insecurity outcomes.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, dataset availability, signal validation with lead-time analysis, false-alarm profiling, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated drought-food-insecurity signal analysis for at least two countries with lead-time profiles and false-alarm rates.

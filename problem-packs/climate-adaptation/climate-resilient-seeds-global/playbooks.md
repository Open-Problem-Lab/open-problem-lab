# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. For adoption-gap mapping: always document adoption-measurement methodology (farmer survey vs. seed sales vs. variety-use observation) and the crop-country specificity of adoption rates.
5. For yield-gap analysis: always document the on-station vs. on-farm distinction — adoption does not guarantee yield advantage under farmer conditions without complementary inputs and extension.
6. For barrier classification: always document farmer-preference interactions — taste, cooking quality, storage, and marketability may matter more to farmers than climate resilience traits alone.
7. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made about climate-resilient seed adoption gaps or seed-system barriers?
2. What source proves it — and is it a national agricultural survey, CGIAR project report, or TASAI infrastructure indicator?
3. What method separates seed-access barriers from farmer-preference barriers, and what is the on-farm yield-gap documentation?
4. What would make it false — particularly if adoption data is outdated, if yield-gap claims don't hold under farmer conditions, or if barrier classification ignores farmer rational preferences?
5. Who could misuse it — particularly to justify seed subsidies that crowd out private distribution, or to promise yield improvement that on-farm conditions cannot deliver?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether an adoption-gap map is impressive. Ask whether it would change a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For climate-resilient seeds, ask specifically:

- Would a CIMMYT seed-system program manager use this to target seed-multiplication investment, or would it be used to justify blanket subsidies that crowd out private-sector distribution?
- Would a national agricultural research director use this to prioritize breeding targets, or would it ignore farmer preferences for taste and marketability?
- Would an agricultural extension officer use this to target outreach, or would it promise yield improvement that complementary inputs cannot support?

The answer must include explicit non-use cases before any field-facing output is published.

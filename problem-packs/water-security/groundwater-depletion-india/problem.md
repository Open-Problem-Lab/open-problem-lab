# Groundwater Depletion Risk Assessment In India

## Problem Statement

India's aquifers are being depleted faster than any credible recharge rate, driven by subsidized electricity, crop price supports for water-intensive cereals, and 30 million tube wells. GRACE satellite gravimetry can see the mass loss from space. But knowing depletion is happening is not the same as knowing where it will become operationally catastrophic and when.

> Which aquifer-level and district-level groundwater depletion signals are evidence-backed enough to deserve replication and water-management review?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: NASA GRACE satellite data shows northern India's groundwater storage declining by approximately 109 cubic kilometers between 2002 and 2016, equivalent to twice the volume of Lake Mead.
- Verified fact: A 2023 Nature study found that over 60 percent of India's irrigated agriculture depends on groundwater, and well depths are increasing by meters per year in hotspot districts of Punjab, Rajasthan, and Tamil Nadu.
- Verified fact: The Central Ground Water Board of India publishes annual groundwater resource assessments at the assessment-unit level with recharge, extraction, and stage-of-development data.
- Verified fact: Electricity subsidies for agricultural pumping are a well-documented driver of groundwater extraction, with near-zero marginal cost of pumping in several states.

## Uncertain Areas

- GRACE spatial resolution limits disaggregation below approximately 300 km footprints.
- Local well-level data is often fragmented, inconsistently digitized, and politically sensitive.
- Whether depletion signals can be verified at scales useful for an individual block or district decision-maker.
- How recharge variability from monsoon rainfall and managed aquifer recharge projects interact with long-term depletion trends.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Evidence inventory and source quality assessment.
- Dataset availability including GRACE, well records, agricultural census, and electricity data.
- Aquifer-level and district-level baseline construction.
- Reproducible depletion signal models with uncertainty ranges.
- Red-team review of false alarms and harmful operational interpretations.
- Field-reality review with Indian water-management and agricultural context.

This pack does not accept water allocation orders, cropping mandates, electricity pricing proposals, or unvalidated groundwater forecasts.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated aquifer-level or district-level depletion signal analysis, with clear limitations, that a domain reviewer judges suitable for further discussion rather than policy action.

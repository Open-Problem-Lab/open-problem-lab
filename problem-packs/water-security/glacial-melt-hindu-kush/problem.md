# Glacial Melt Water Security Risk In The Hindu Kush Himalaya

## Problem Statement

The Hindu Kush Himalaya is the world's third pole — the largest ice mass outside the Arctic and Antarctic. Its glaciers feed ten of Asia's greatest rivers, supplying water to 2 billion people for drinking, irrigation, and hydropower. ICIMOD's landmark 2019 assessment found the region could lose one-third to two-thirds of its glaciers by 2100. The question is not whether glaciers are melting — they are. The question is which river basins face the most acute water-security risk and when, given the compound uncertainty of melt rates, precipitation change, and downstream demand growth.

> Which Hindu Kush Himalaya river basins face the highest evidence-backed glacial-melt water security risk at which time horizons, with uncertainty honest enough for adaptation planning?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: ICIMOD's 2019 Hindu Kush Himalaya Assessment found the region could lose 36-64 percent of glacier volume by 2100 under high-emission scenarios, with accelerated mass loss already observed across all major ranges.
- Verified fact: Glaciers contribute 30-60 percent of dry-season river flow in the Indus, Ganges, and Brahmaputra basins, making seasonal water availability acutely sensitive to melt timing changes.
- Verified fact: Permafrost thaw, snowpack decline, and glacial lake outburst floods compound glacial-melt risks in ways poorly captured by melt-rate projections alone.
- Verified fact: Downstream water demand is rising rapidly due to population growth, agricultural intensification, and industrialization across South and East Asia.

## Uncertain Areas

- Precipitation change projections diverge across models and scenarios for the monsoon-influenced eastern Himalaya.
- Glacier mass-balance observations are sparse for many sub-ranges and require satellite and model interpolation.
- How downstream water management infrastructure and demand interact with upstream melt changes is basin-specific and poorly modeled.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on: evidence inventory, dataset availability, basin-scale melt-projection comparison, water-security risk ranking with scenario uncertainty, red-team review, field-reality review.

This pack does not accept water-allocation recommendations, dam-building proposals, or unvalidated water-scarcity forecasts.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated basin-scale glacial-melt water-security risk analysis for at least three river basins, with explicit scenario uncertainty, suitable for adaptation discussion.

# Wildfire Smoke PM2.5 And Health

## Problem Statement

Wildfire smoke contributes up to 25 percent of global PM2.5 and is increasing. Smoke PM2.5 may be more toxic than other sources. NOAA HMS smoke plumes, GEOS-Chem composition, and satellite AOD data exist — but linking smoke-specific PM2.5 to verified respiratory health outcomes is underdeveloped.

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: A 2021 Lancet Planetary Health study estimated wildfire smoke contributes up to 25 percent of global ambient PM2.5, with smoke PM2.5 potentially 2-3x more toxic per unit than other PM2.5 sources.
- Verified fact: NOAA Hazard Mapping System provides daily satellite-derived smoke-plume polygons going back to 2006. GEOS-Chem provides smoke-specific PM2.5 composition estimates.
- Verified fact: Wildfire-smoke days are associated with 5-15 percent increases in respiratory emergency department visits and 1-3 percent increases in all-cause mortality.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated smoke-exposure health impact analysis for at least two fire-prone regions.

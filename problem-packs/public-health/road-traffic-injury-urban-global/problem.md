# Road Traffic Injury Mortality Risk In LMICs

## Problem Statement

Road traffic kills 1.3 million people annually — the leading cause of death for people aged 5-29. Over 90 percent occur in LMICs. A crash victim's survival depends on reaching a trauma facility within the golden hour. But in the cities where crashes are most lethal, verified maps of crash locations and trauma-care response times are nearly nonexistent.

> Which urban areas in LMICs have the highest evidence-backed gap between road traffic injury mortality risk and emergency trauma care access?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO Global Status Report on Road Safety 2023 estimates 1.3 million annual road traffic deaths, over 90 percent in LMICs, with the highest death rates in Africa and Southeast Asia.
- Verified fact: Post-crash care is one of WHO's five pillars of road safety — survival probability drops sharply after 60 minutes without definitive care.
- Verified fact: Many LMIC cities lack systematic crash-location data, relying on police reports that undercount non-fatal crashes and lack geographic coordinates.
- Verified fact: OpenStreetMap road-network data and Mapillary street-level imagery increasingly enable road-safety auditing without in-person surveys.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether police crash-report data can be supplemented with hospital trauma-registry data to produce reliable crash-location maps, how informal trauma care and private ambulance services change effective access, and whether risk maps change actual road-safety or trauma-system investment decisions.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, crash-location data compilation, trauma-facility mapping, access-gap analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated urban road-trauma access gap analysis for at least two LMIC cities.

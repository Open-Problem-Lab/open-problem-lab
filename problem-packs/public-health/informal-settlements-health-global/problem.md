# Informal Settlement Health Access Gaps

## Problem Statement

1 billion people live in informal settlements, projected to reach 3 billion by 2050. Residents face compounded health risks — overcrowding, poor WASH, and no health facilities — but are invisible in official statistics. Satellite-derived settlement maps at 10m resolution now exist from the IDEAMAPS project and others. Connecting these maps to health-facility access data at neighborhood scale is the missing tool for urban health equity.

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: UN-Habitat estimates 1 billion people live in informal settlements, projected to reach 3 billion by 2050 if current trends continue.
- Verified fact: Informal-settlement residents have 1.5-3x higher child mortality, 2-5x higher infectious disease burden, and substantially lower health-service utilization than formal urban residents.
- Verified fact: IDEAMAPS and other projects now produce machine-learning-derived informal-settlement boundary maps at 10m resolution for major LMIC cities.
- Verified fact: DHS surveys provide urban cluster health data but do not disaggregate informal versus formal settlement populations.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether ML-derived settlement maps accurately distinguish informal from formal areas across diverse urban contexts. Whether survey data can be disaggregated to settlement level. Whether access maps change urban health planning.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, settlement-boundary compilation, health-facility access analysis, gap mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated informal-settlement health access gap analysis for at least two LMIC cities.

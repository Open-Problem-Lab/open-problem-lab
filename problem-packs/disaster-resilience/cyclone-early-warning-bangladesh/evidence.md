## Evidence Notes

### Haque et al. 2012 Mortality Review

Use this source as the landmark evidence for Bangladesh's cyclone mortality reduction success. This establishes the life-saving value of the warning system but does not provide signal-level verification for modern forecast products.

### Alam/Bangladesh CPP Documentation

Use this source for the operational architecture of the Cyclone Preparedness Programme — volunteer numbers, shelter inventory, and warning dissemination methods. The CPP is the bridge between a verified signal and a life-saving action.

### Mohapatra et al. 2020 Track Error Analysis

Use this source for the strongest baseline evidence on Bay of Bengal cyclone track forecast skill. The 50-80 km at 24 hours / 80-120 km at 48 hours error ranges define the spatial resolution problem for district-level evacuation decisions.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

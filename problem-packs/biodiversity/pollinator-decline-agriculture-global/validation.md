# Validation

## Validation Layers

| Layer           | Gate                                                                 |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Structure       | `pnpm validate` passes schemas and problem-pack completeness checks  |
| Evidence        | Every claim has a dated evidence record                              |
| Reproducibility | Quantitative artifacts include rerunnable steps                      |
| Review          | Required reviewer roles approve or request changes                   |
| Replication     | Safety-sensitive or quantitative claims are independently reproduced |

## Baseline Requirements

Sub-national pollination-service analyses must document:

- Crop-pollination dependence classification source and whether derived from temperate or tropical studies.
- MapSPAM or equivalent crop-production data version, resolution, and allocation methodology.
- Satellite land-cover product used with classification accuracy assessment for the analyzed region.
- LMIC data-gap documentation — explicitly state where direct pollinator monitoring data is absent.
- Administrative boundary version and sub-national aggregation method.

## Proxy-Validation Requirements

No pollination-service risk claim may be called actionable until it includes:

- Documentation that the habitat-to-pollinator inference relies on proxy relationships not validated with ground monitoring in the analyzed region.
- Comparison with at least one published study on landscape simplification and pollinator abundance, with explicit geographic applicability assessment.
- Sensitivity analysis showing how risk estimates change under alternative pollination-dependence classifications or habitat-proxy definitions.
- LMIC monitoring-gap documentation stating what direct evidence would be needed to validate the proxy inference.

## Crop-Loss Attribution Requirements

No crop-production loss claim attributed to pollinator decline may be merged without:

- Direct pollinator monitoring data from the analyzed region (not proxy indicators).
- Confound controls for other crop-production drivers (climate, soil, management, market access).
- Explicit statement that correlation between habitat change and crop production does not establish pollinator-mediated causation.
- Non-use case documentation (not for pesticide-ban justification, not for crop-loss insurance claims).

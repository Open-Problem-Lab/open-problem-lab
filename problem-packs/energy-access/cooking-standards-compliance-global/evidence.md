## Evidence Notes

### ISO Clean Cookstoves Standards 2018

Use this source for the standards framework. ISO 19867-1 defines laboratory test protocols but does not mandate independent verification — manufacturers can self-certify. Compliance-verification systems vary enormously by country. The standards define lab protocols that may not reflect field conditions. No global compliance database exists. Always document verification-system coverage and lab-vs-field limitations.

### Lab-Field Performance Degradation

Use this source for the strongest evidence on performance degradation. The 30-70 percent range is across multiple stove categories and contexts. Field studies use different measurement methods than lab tests — some apparent degradation may reflect method differences. Long-term degradation is less studied. Always present with stove-category specificity and measurement-method caveats.

### Household Air Pollution Burden

Use this source for the health-burden baseline. The 3.2 million figure is a modeled estimate from exposure-response functions. Adoption rates are self-reported — 'adoption' may mean ownership rather than exclusive use. The burden estimate does not distinguish partial from complete fuel transition. Always document these limitations in health-impact claims.

### Field Monitoring Coverage

Use this source to document the fundamental evidence constraint: field-performance monitoring covers fewer than 5 percent of deployed stoves. Health-impact estimates are based on lab data and exposure models, not verified field measurements. Monitoring methodology varies. Always document this coverage gap explicitly.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

## Evidence Notes

### World Bank Groundswell 2021

Use this source with the critical caveat that the 40 million figure is a modeled projection under pessimistic scenarios, not an observed migration trend or a forecast of actual migration. The gravity-based migration model simplifies the interaction between climate and economic drivers and does not capture migration barriers. The figure is a regional aggregate with substantial scenario-dependent variation. Always present as a scenario projection, not as a prediction.

### Rigaud Environmental Migration Review 2021

Use this source to document that environmental-migration correlations exist but are modest — economic drivers explain more migration variance than environmental signals. The correlations reflect long-term patterns from decadal census data, not event-driven displacement. Attribution to climate versus economic drivers is inherently uncertain because environmental and economic stressors co-occur. Always document the marginal explanatory power of environmental signals relative to economic drivers.

### Census Migration Data Limitations

Use this source to document the fundamental data constraint: South Asian census migration definitions vary across countries (place of birth vs. last residence vs. duration), censuses are decadal, and short-term and irregular migration is undercounted. Cross-country comparison requires explicit harmonization. Census data captures net migration — it does not capture return, circular, or step-wise migration chains. Always document these limitations in correlation analyses.

### Satellite Environmental Migration Correlation

Use this source to document that satellite environmental indicators (NDVI, rainfall anomalies) correlate with migration but explain only 5-15 percent of variance — 85-95 percent is explained by non-environmental factors. The environmental signal may proxy for agricultural income loss rather than causing migration directly. Spatial autocorrelation can inflate correlations if not controlled. Always present with explicit variance-explained documentation and non-causal caveats.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

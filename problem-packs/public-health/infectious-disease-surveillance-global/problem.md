# Infectious Disease Surveillance Timeliness And Completeness Gaps In Low-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

Build a verified workflow for measuring infectious disease surveillance timeliness and completeness, linking WHO IHR monitoring data and national surveillance data.

> Which evidence-backed signals are sufficient to support intervention targeting without overstating certainty?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO IHR monitoring reports that fewer than 50 percent of low-income countries meet core surveillance capacity requirements, with reporting timeliness below 30 percent in several countries.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether available data is sufficient for sub-national analysis. Whether gap maps change actual policy or investment decisions. How measurement methodology compares across countries.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, dataset compilation, sub-national gap analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national analysis with explicit uncertainty documentation.

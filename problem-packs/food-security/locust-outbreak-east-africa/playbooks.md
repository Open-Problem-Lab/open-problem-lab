# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. Keep quantitative outputs reproducible.
5. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What outbreak claim is being made?
2. What source proves the signal-lead-time relationship?
3. What method produced the estimate?
4. What would make the outbreak signal a false alarm?
5. Who could misuse an outbreak risk map to justify harmful pesticide use or displacement?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a vegetation anomaly is statistically significant. Ask whether a locust control officer would trust the signal enough to reposition teams and equipment, and what would go wrong if the signal was false. For East Africa, ask specifically whether the output would work in the actual field-reporting environment including conflict zones, poor road access, and limited spray aircraft availability.

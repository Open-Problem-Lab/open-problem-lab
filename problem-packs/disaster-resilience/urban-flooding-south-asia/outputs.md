## Candidate Output Types

- Evidence inventory with satellite impervious-surface product comparison and drainage-data availability assessment for South Asian megacities.
- Neighborhood-scale impervious-surface map for at least two South Asian megacities with change-over-time analysis.
- Drainage-capacity gap analysis combining available drainage data with impervious-surface proxies.
- Neighborhood-scale pluvial flood-risk map validated against historical flood-extent data.
- Failure-mode review of flood-map misuse for eviction and displacement.
- Field-reality review with municipal drainage and urban-planning context.

## Publication Rule

Published outputs must say what they are not. A flood-risk map is not a drainage-infrastructure recommendation, a land-use regulation basis, or justification for informal-settlement eviction. Flood maps must be presented as tools for infrastructure investment prioritization, not displacement. Drainage-data limitations must be explicit — where drainage data is absent, the risk map reflects impervious-surface proxies, not verified drainage inadequacy.

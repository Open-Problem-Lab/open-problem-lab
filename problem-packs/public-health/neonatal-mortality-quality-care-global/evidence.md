## Evidence Notes

### UNICEF Levels and Trends in Child Mortality 2024

Use this source for the neonatal mortality baseline — 2.3 million neonatal deaths in 2022, with Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia accounting for over 80 percent. Three causes — prematurity (35 percent), birth asphyxia (20 percent), and neonatal sepsis (15 percent) — are responsible for 70 percent of deaths. All are responsive to quality inpatient newborn care.

Cause-of-death attribution depends on verbal autopsy where civil registration is incomplete, which is the case in most high-burden countries. Sub-national mortality estimates are modeled using small-area estimation methods and have substantial uncertainty for small populations. Do not use national neonatal mortality rates as a substitute for district-level estimates.

### Service Provision Assessment (SPA) Context

SPA surveys assess facility readiness across multiple domains and are the most comprehensive source of inpatient newborn care quality data in LMICs. However, SPA surveys are conducted infrequently (approximately every 5-10 years per country) and facility-level data may not reflect current conditions. Facilities classified as providing inpatient newborn care in older surveys may have improved or deteriorated without documentation. The seven signal functions — neonatal resuscitation, CPAP, thermal care, IV fluids, IV antibiotics, phototherapy, and safe oxygen delivery — are the standard for classifying a facility as capable of providing quality inpatient newborn care.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

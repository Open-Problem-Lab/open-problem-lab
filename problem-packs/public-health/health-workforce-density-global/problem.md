# Health Workforce Density And Skill-Mix Gaps In Low-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

WHO projects a 10 million health worker shortage by 2030, concentrated in the poorest countries. 80 countries fall below the 4.45 per 1000 density target. National workforce registries track who is licensed — but sub-national distribution mapping showing which districts have what types of health workers is almost universally absent from countries with the most severe shortages.

> Which sub-national areas in low-income countries have the most severe evidence-backed health workforce density and skill-mix gaps?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO projects a global shortage of 10 million health workers by 2030, with 80 countries below the 4.45 per 1000 density threshold, concentrated in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia.
- Verified fact: National health workforce registries and payroll data provide worker counts and cadres but rarely include geo-located deployment data.
- Verified fact: WHO NHWA platform collects workforce data at national level but sub-national distribution data is reported by fewer than 30 countries.
- Verified fact: Skill-mix gaps — ratio of specialists to generalists, nurses to doctors — are as important as absolute density but are rarely mapped.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether payroll data can be geo-located to create sub-national workforce maps. Whether density maps change recruitment, deployment, or retention policy.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, workforce registry compilation, density and skill-mix gap mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national workforce gap analysis for at least two low-income countries.

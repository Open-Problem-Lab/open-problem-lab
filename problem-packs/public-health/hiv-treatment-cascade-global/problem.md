# HIV Treatment Cascade Gaps In Sub-Saharan Africa

## Problem Statement

UNAIDS 95-95-95 targets. Sub-Saharan Africa has the world's largest HIV treatment programs. PHIA surveys measure the cascade at population level every 3-5 years — gold standard, but slow. Routine program data arrives monthly but undercounts patients lost to follow-up and lacks viral-load data for many sites. Reconciling these two data streams at district scale is the practical measurement problem blocking program improvement.

> Which districts in Sub-Saharan Africa have the largest evidence-backed gaps between PHIA-survey cascade estimates and routine program data?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: UNAIDS estimates 39 million people living with HIV globally, 25 million in Sub-Saharan Africa. 86 percent know their status, 76 percent are on treatment, 71 percent are virally suppressed — still short of 95-95-95.
- Verified fact: PHIA surveys provide population-based HIV cascade estimates at district level and are the gold standard for cascade measurement, but are conducted every 3-5 years in a limited number of countries.
- Verified fact: Routine PEPFAR and national program data provides monthly treatment-initiation and retention data but systematically undercounts patients lost to follow-up who have silently transferred or died.
- Verified fact: Viral-load testing coverage averages 60-70 percent of patients on treatment in SSA, meaning viral suppression estimates from routine data exclude a substantial proportion of treated patients.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether PHIA-to-routine-data reconciliation methods can produce valid sub-annual cascade estimates. Whether lost-to-follow-up patients can be traced through multi-facility record linkage. Whether cascade gap maps change program targeting.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, cascade data compilation, PHIA-routine reconciliation, district gap mapping, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national HIV cascade gap analysis for at least two SSA countries.

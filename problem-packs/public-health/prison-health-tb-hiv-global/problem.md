# Prison Health Tuberculosis And HIV Service Gaps In Low- And Middle-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

Prison populations have tuberculosis notification rates 10-100 times higher than the general population and HIV prevalence 2-10 times higher — driven by overcrowding, poor ventilation, delayed diagnosis, interrupted treatment, and high pre-incarceration risk. Prisons function as epidemiological pumps: undiagnosed infections acquired or untreated inside prisons spill into communities upon release, which occurs for an estimated 30 million people annually. The 2015 Nelson Mandela Rules, adopted by the UN General Assembly, mandate that prison health services be equivalent to community health standards. Yet prison health data is systematically excluded from national health management information systems in most low- and middle-income countries.

A 2021 Lancet review found that fewer than 30 LMICs report any prison health data to WHO, and fewer than 10 disaggregate by individual prison facility. The World Prison Brief provides prison-population estimates for most countries, but facility-level population, occupancy, and health-service data are almost universally absent from public datasets. Mapping where prison populations face the highest TB and HIV burden with the lowest service access requires triangulating sparse prevalence studies, facility-level reports, and modeled estimates — a methodological challenge that has not been tackled at sub-national scale.

> Which prison facilities and sub-national jurisdictions in LMICs have the highest evidence-backed gap between estimated TB/HIV disease burden and documented screening, treatment, and continuity-of-care service access?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO and the 2021 Lancet prison-health review estimate that prison TB notification rates are 10-100 times higher than civilian rates, with the highest ratios in overcrowded pre-trial detention facilities in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia.
- Verified fact: HIV prevalence in prison populations is 2-10 times higher than in surrounding communities, driven by injection-drug use, sexual transmission, and high pre-incarceration prevalence among key populations.
- Verified fact: The Nelson Mandela Rules (UN General Assembly Resolution 70/175, 2015) mandate equivalence of prison health services with community standards, including TB diagnosis and treatment, HIV testing and antiretroviral therapy, and continuity of care upon release. However, fewer than 30 LMICs include prison health indicators in routine reporting.
- Verified fact: Prison release rates — approximately 30 million people per year globally — mean that undiagnosed and untreated TB and HIV in prisons create ongoing community transmission risk, particularly in countries with high incarceration rates and weak post-release health-system linkage.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether facility-level prison-population, occupancy, and health-service data can be ethically accessed for analysis without compromising detainee privacy or security protocols.
- How TB and HIV prevalence in prison populations should be estimated where screening and prevalence-survey data are absent — can community-prevalence estimates adjusted for incarceration risk factors produce usable burden estimates?
- Whether prison-health service-gap analyses that reveal poor conditions can be conducted without being used to justify further neglect or punitive measures against incarcerated populations.
- How continuity-of-care from prison to community post-release can be measured when both prison and community health information systems exclude release-status tracking.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Evidence inventory and source-quality assessment for prison-population data, TB/HIV prevalence studies in prisons, and health-service-access data.
- Prison-population compilation from World Prison Brief, national prison-administration reports, and published prevalence studies.
- TB/HIV disease-burden estimation for prison populations at sub-national scale with explicit uncertainty documentation.
- Health-service access-gap analysis comparing estimated burden to documented screening, treatment, and continuity-of-care coverage.
- Red-team review of privacy, stigmatization, and punitive-use risks.
- Field-reality review with prison-health authorities and detainee-rights organizations.

This pack does not accept data collection within prison facilities without documented ethical review and informed consent, health-service recommendations that could worsen detainee conditions, or analyses that identify individual facilities in ways that create security or retribution risks.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated prison-health TB/HIV service-gap analysis for at least two LMICs, with explicit data-availability documentation, privacy and security safeguards, and facility-identification protocols reviewed and approved by an independent ethical reviewer.

## Evidence Notes

### WHO/UNICEF WUENIC 2024

Use this source for the global immunization coverage baseline — 20 million zero-dose and under-vaccinated children in 2023, concentrated in ten countries (Nigeria, India, Pakistan, Ethiopia, DRC, Indonesia, Angola, Yemen, Afghanistan, and Brazil) and disproportionately in the poorest wealth quintile and remote rural areas. The WUENIC estimates are modeled from DHS/MICS survey data and administrative reports, and sub-national estimates are not systematically reported.

Do not use the national zero-dose figure as a substitute for sub-national coverage-equity analysis. DHS and MICS surveys provide the underlying data for sub-national estimation, but survey frequency (3-5 years) limits the temporal resolution of zero-dose tracking. Districts with fewer than 500 live births in the survey sample should be flagged as having wide confidence intervals for zero-dose estimates.

### Immunization Agenda 2030 Context

The Immunization Agenda 2030 targets 50 percent reduction in zero-dose children globally by 2030. Achieving this requires routinely identifying where zero-dose children live and which barriers prevent their vaccination — wealth, geography, gender, and health-system access. Current surveillance systems do not systematically report zero-dose children by these dimensions at sub-national scale.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

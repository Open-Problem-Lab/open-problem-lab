# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. Keep quantitative outputs reproducible.
5. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What risk factor or exposure claim is being made?
2. What source proves the spatial relationship?
3. What method produced the risk map?
4. What would make the risk map dangerously wrong?
5. Who could misuse a risk map to stigmatize a neighborhood or displace residents?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether the risk map looks plausible. Ask whether a ministry of health official would use it to decide where to place blood-lead screening, and what would happen if the map flagged the wrong neighborhood. Lead risk maps can trigger property value loss, resident displacement, and community blame. Every output must include explicit non-use cases and guardrails against stigmatization.

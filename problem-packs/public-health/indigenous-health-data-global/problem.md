# Indigenous Health Data Sovereignty And Service Access Gaps

## Problem Statement

Indigenous populations die 5-20 years younger than non-indigenous populations in every country with data. Yet indigenous identifiers are absent from most national health systems. Where data exists, data sovereignty principles demand indigenous governance. Measuring health gaps without colonial data extraction is the methodological challenge.

> How can indigenous health service access gaps be measured while respecting CARE principles and indigenous data sovereignty?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: UN Permanent Forum on Indigenous Issues documents life-expectancy gaps of 5-20 years between indigenous and non-indigenous populations across all countries with data.
- Verified fact: Fewer than 30 countries include indigenous identifiers in routine health data, and fewer than 10 have indigenous-governed health data systems.
- Verified fact: CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance (Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, Ethics) have been endorsed by the UN and research-funding bodies.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated indigenous health access gap analysis co-designed with indigenous data governance.

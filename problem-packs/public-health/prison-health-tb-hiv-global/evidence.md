## Evidence Notes

### Dolan et al. 2021 Lancet Prison Health Review

Use this source for the systematic review evidence on prison TB and HIV burden — prison TB notification rates are 10-100 times higher than civilian rates, and prison HIV prevalence is 2-10 times higher than surrounding communities. The review found that fewer than 30 LMICs report any prison health data to WHO, and fewer than 10 disaggregate by prison facility.

Prison health data from the studies included in this review is sparse and likely underestimates true burden in facilities without active screening. Studies are concentrated in a small number of countries — South Africa, Brazil, India, Thailand, and Ukraine — and may not generalize to other LMIC prison systems. Do not use the 10-100x or 2-10x ranges as precise multipliers for any specific facility without local data.

### Nelson Mandela Rules and Prison Health Governance Context

Rule 24 of the Nelson Mandela Rules (UN General Assembly Resolution 70/175, 2015) mandates that "Prisoners should enjoy the same standards of health care that are available in the community." In practice, prison health services in most LMICs are administered by ministries of interior or justice rather than ministries of health, creating structural barriers to health-system integration. Continuity of care upon release — particularly for TB treatment and antiretroviral therapy — is systematically absent from both prison and community health information systems.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

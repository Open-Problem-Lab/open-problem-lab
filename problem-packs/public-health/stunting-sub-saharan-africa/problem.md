# Child Stunting Hotspot Identification In Sub-Saharan Africa

## Problem Statement

Stunting affects 149 million children globally. Sub-Saharan Africa carries 40 percent of the burden and is the only region where absolute numbers are rising. Stunting before age two causes irreversible cognitive damage. The interventions work — maternal nutrition, breastfeeding support, complementary feeding, infection control — but reaching the right children requires knowing where the persistently highest rates are. Sub-national stunting data in the highest-burden countries is often five years old, from different survey rounds using different methodologies, making reliable hotspot identification deceptively difficult.

> Which sub-national child stunting hotspots in Sub-Saharan Africa are evidence-backed enough across multiple survey rounds to support nutrition intervention targeting?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO, UNICEF, and World Bank estimate 149 million stunted children under five globally, 40 percent in Sub-Saharan Africa, with stunting rates exceeding 30 percent in many districts and exceeding 50 percent in several countries.
- Verified fact: The DHS and MICS surveys provide district-level stunting estimates but survey rounds are spaced 3-5 years apart, methodology changes between rounds, and some high-burden countries lack recent data.
- Verified fact: A 2021 Lancet series estimated that scaling ten evidence-based nutrition interventions to 90 percent coverage could reduce stunting by 20 percent, but current intervention coverage in high-burden districts is typically below 30 percent.
- Verified fact: Stunting is associated with food insecurity, poor dietary diversity, repeated infection, inadequate water and sanitation, and poverty — all of which interact and vary sub-nationally.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether survey-to-survey comparability problems can be overcome to identify persistent versus transient stunting hotspots.
- Whether intervention coverage data is accurate enough at district scale to identify coverage gaps.
- How food-security shocks interact with long-term stunting trends at district scale.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on: evidence inventory, dataset availability, sub-national stunting rate estimation with data-quality tiering, persistent hotspot classification, intervention coverage gap analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

This pack does not accept nutrition program budget recommendations, intervention mandates, or unvalidated stunting forecasts.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated sub-national stunting hotspot analysis using at least two survey rounds, with explicit data-quality documentation, suitable for program discussion.

# Severe Acute Malnutrition Wasting Detection And Treatment Coverage In Sub-Saharan Africa

## Problem Statement

Severe acute malnutrition kills over half a million children under five every year. Ready-to-use therapeutic food can treat 90 percent of SAM cases — but only if the child is identified and reaches a treatment program. In Sub-Saharan Africa, treatment coverage averages below 30 percent. The gap between the number of children with SAM in a district and the number receiving treatment is the single most actionable metric for reducing child mortality from malnutrition. Yet district-level treatment coverage gaps are rarely systematically verified.

> Which districts in Sub-Saharan Africa have the highest evidence-backed gap between severe acute malnutrition prevalence and treatment program coverage?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: UNICEF, WHO, and World Bank estimate 45 million children under five suffer from wasting globally, of whom 13.6 million have severe acute malnutrition, with Sub-Saharan Africa carrying a disproportionate share.
- Verified fact: Community-based management of acute malnutrition using ready-to-use therapeutic foods can achieve recovery rates above 90 percent when coverage exceeds 70 percent. Actual coverage in most Sub-Saharan African countries is below 30 percent.
- Verified fact: SMART surveys and DHS data provide periodic district-level wasting estimates, but surveys are irregular, and treatment program data from national health management information systems often undercount children reached through NGO and community-based programs.
- Verified fact: The 2021 Global Nutrition Report found that only 6 of 51 Sub-Saharan African countries were on track to meet the World Health Assembly target for reducing childhood wasting by 2025.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether survey-based prevalence estimates and HMIS treatment-count data can be reconciled at district scale.
- How seasonal variation in wasting prevalence interacts with treatment program capacity.
- Whether treatment coverage gaps identified from data change actual program resource allocation.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, dataset availability, district-level SAM burden estimation, treatment coverage gap analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated district-level SAM treatment coverage gap analysis for at least two countries, with explicit data-reconciliation methodology.

# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. For productive-use gap mapping: always document the value-chain specificity — milling, drying, cold storage, irrigation, and processing have different electricity-demand requirements and adoption barriers.
5. For income-impact analysis: always document context-dependency — electrification in isolated communities without market access may have near-zero productive-use impact. Present with confound controls for market infrastructure and human capital.
6. For access-vs-use distinction: always document that electricity access does not equal verified productive use — equipment ownership does not equal actual productive use frequency.
7. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made about productive use or income impact from electrification?
2. What source proves it — and is it mini-grid operator data, an enterprise survey, or an agricultural value-chain study?
3. What method translates electricity access into productive-use or income claims, and what is the access-vs-use distinction?
4. What would make it false — particularly if productive-use demand doesn't materialize at projected rates, if context-dependency is lost in generalization, or if projected rather than observed productive-use data drives investment?
5. Who could misuse it — particularly to project income gains without observed productive-use evidence, to overgeneralize context-dependent results, or to drive mini-grid investment without productive-use demand validation?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a productive-use gap map is impressive. Ask whether it would change a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For productive use of energy in LMICs, ask specifically:

- Would a Kenya Rural Electrification Authority productive-use officer use this to target mini-grid site selection for agricultural value chains, or would projected income gains drive investment without observed productive-use evidence?
- Would a mini-grid operator use this to justify productive-use tariff structures, or would context-dependent results be overgeneralized across sites with different market conditions?
- Would an agricultural extension officer use this to promote electrified processing, or would equipment adoption barriers (affordability, maintenance, market access) be underestimated?

The answer must include explicit non-use cases before any field-facing output is published.

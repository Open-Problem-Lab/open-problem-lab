# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. Keep quantitative outputs reproducible.
5. Mark every adaptation-prioritization claim as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What SLR or coastal exposure claim is being made?
2. What projection, elevation, and population data support it?
3. What method produced the exposure estimate?
4. What would make the exposure estimate dangerously wrong?
5. Who could misuse an exposure map to justify forced relocation or denial of coastal development rights?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether SLR is an existential threat — it is. Ask whether a named adaptation planner in a named SIDS would use this output to change their National Adaptation Plan or coastal infrastructure investment, and what would happen if the local SLR or erosion projection was wrong. The worst outcome is directing scarce adaptation funds toward a projection that seems precise but is locally wrong, while ignoring a less-quantified but more urgent threat like saltwater intrusion or coral-reef degradation.

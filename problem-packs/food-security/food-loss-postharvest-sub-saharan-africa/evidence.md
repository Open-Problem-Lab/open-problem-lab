## Evidence Notes

### FAO Food Loss Index 2023

Use this source for the regional loss baseline. The 30-40 percent figure is a regional aggregate — sub-national estimates are not available for most countries. Loss data is sparse and modeled. Loss-stage classification depends on survey methodology that varies across countries. The FAO index combines multiple sources with different measurement approaches. Always document these limitations in sub-national analyses.

### APHLIS Postharvest Loss 2023

Use APHLIS for the best available sub-national cereal loss estimates. APHLIS covers cereals only — perishable crops with higher loss rates are not included. Estimates are modeled from survey data and expert assessments, not directly measured. Model inputs vary in quality across provinces. Aggregate loss estimates mask loss-stage detail. Always document crop coverage and measurement limitations.

### Hermetic Storage Effectiveness

Use this source for loss-reduction potential. The 50-80 percent reduction range is from controlled trials — on-farm reduction may be lower from improper sealing and pest re-infestation. Adoption below 10 percent is a continental average. Cost barriers vary by product type. The review does not capture perishable crop solutions. Always present with adoption-barrier and on-farm-vs-trial limitations.

### Cold Chain Infrastructure Gap

Use this source to document the perishable-crop constraint. Cold-chain capacity data is sparse — most SSA countries lack systematic refrigeration inventories. The 30-50 percent higher perishable loss is a general estimate. Solar and evaporative cooling solutions are at pilot scale. Informal cold-chain practices may partially reduce losses. Always document data-sparsity and solution-scalability limitations.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

# Urban Lead Exposure Risk Mapping In Low- And Middle-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

Lead exposure is a neglected environmental health crisis. An estimated 800 million children globally have blood lead levels above 5 micrograms per deciliter. The damage to cognitive development, educational attainment, and lifetime earnings is well-documented and irreversible. Yet neighborhood-scale risk mapping — the kind that lets a health ministry target interventions — is nearly absent from the cities where the burden is highest.

> Which urban-scale lead exposure risk factors and data sources are evidence-backed enough to support neighborhood-level risk mapping in low- and middle-income countries?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: UNICEF and Pure Earth report that approximately 1 in 3 children globally have elevated blood lead levels, with 90 percent of the burden concentrated in low- and middle-income countries.
- Verified fact: The WHO identifies lead as one of ten chemicals of major public health concern, with no known safe blood level for children.
- Verified fact: Major exposure pathways in LMICs include informal lead-acid battery recycling, lead-contaminated spices, lead-based paint, ceramic cookware, mining, and electronic waste processing.
- Verified fact: The Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation estimates lead exposure caused over 5.5 million cardiovascular disease deaths and 900,000 intellectual disability-attributable deaths in 2019.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether open-source spatial data can identify lead risk hotspots at neighborhood scale without primary sampling.
- How formal and informal industrial facilities data can be combined to estimate proximity-based lead exposure risk.
- Whether mapped risk correlates with actual blood lead measurements where those measurements exist.
- How risk maps would be used by health ministries without causing stigmatization or displacement.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Evidence inventory and source quality assessment.
- Dataset availability including industrial permits, informal sector data, satellite imagery, and blood-lead studies.
- Risk factor spatial inventory at city scale.
- Reproducible risk mapping methodology with validation against blood-lead data where available.
- Red-team review of misuse including stigmatization and displacement risk.
- Field-reality review with public-health implementation context.

This pack does not accept blood-lead screening mandates, individual health advice, or unvalidated risk scores presented as definitive exposure measurements.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated urban neighborhood-level lead risk factor map, with explicit validation limitations, that a domain reviewer judges suitable for further discussion rather than screening decisions.

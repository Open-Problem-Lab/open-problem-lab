## Candidate Output Types

- Evidence inventory with WFP coverage data granularity assessment and meal-quality documentation.
- Sub-national school feeding coverage map for at least two low-income countries with data-granularity documentation.
- Nutritional-impact analysis linking coverage to anthropometric outcomes with meal-quality variation and enrollment-vs-nutrition distinction.
- Failure-mode review of coverage overstatement and nutritional-impact exaggeration.
- Field-reality review with education ministry and WFP program context.

## Publication Rule

Published outputs must say what they are not. A coverage map is not a nutritional-impact measurement — coverage without meal-quality data does not demonstrate nutritional improvement. National statistics mask district-level gaps. Enrollment effects (strong evidence) must be distinguished from nutritional effects (weaker evidence).

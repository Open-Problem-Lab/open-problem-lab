# Renewable Energy Grid Integration Bottlenecks In LMICs

## Problem Statement

Solar and wind are now the cheapest new electricity sources globally. LMICs receive only 15 percent of renewable energy investment. The bottleneck is not resource or cost — it is grid absorption capacity, regulatory barriers, and financing access. Knowing which grids can integrate what amount of renewables at what cost is the missing link.

> Which LMIC electricity grids face the highest evidence-backed renewable energy integration bottlenecks, and which bottlenecks are most tractable?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: IEA World Energy Outlook 2024 reports solar PV is the cheapest electricity source in history, but LMICs receive only 15 percent of global clean energy investment despite hosting two-thirds of the global population.
- Verified fact: Grid-integration studies from IRENA and national utilities show that many LMIC grids can absorb 15-30 percent variable renewable energy without major upgrades, but absorption capacity varies dramatically by grid size, generation mix, and interconnection.
- Verified fact: Regulatory barriers — including monopoly utilities, below-cost tariffs, and lack of independent power producer frameworks — are cited as primary obstacles in investor surveys across Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether grid-absorption studies are technically comparable across countries. Whether regulatory-barrier classification translates into actionable reform recommendations. Whether bottleneck maps change investor or utility behavior.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, grid-capacity data compilation, renewable-resource mapping, bottleneck classification, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated grid-integration bottleneck analysis for at least three LMIC electricity systems.

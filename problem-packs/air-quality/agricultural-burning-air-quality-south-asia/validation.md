# Validation

## Validation Layers

| Layer           | Gate                                                                 |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Structure       | `pnpm validate` passes schemas and problem-pack completeness checks  |
| Evidence        | Every claim has a dated evidence record                              |
| Reproducibility | Quantitative artifacts include rerunnable steps                      |
| Review          | Required reviewer roles approve or request changes                   |
| Replication     | Safety-sensitive or quantitative claims are independently reproduced |

## Baseline Requirements

Sub-national agricultural-burning analyses must document:

- Fire-detection product used (MODIS vs. VIIRS) with resolution and known detection gaps.
- Cloud-cover masking methodology for the October-November monsoon withdrawal period.
- Ground-monitor selection rationale and spatial representativeness of CPCB stations.
- Non-burning-season baseline period for seasonal comparison.
- Administrative boundary version and district-level aggregation method.

## Source-Attribution Requirements

No agricultural-burning-to-PM2.5 attribution claim may be called actionable until it includes:

- Source-apportionment methodology (chemical tracer, receptor model, satellite-based, or hybrid).
- Comparison with at least one published source-apportionment study for the same region.
- Explicit documentation of confound controls for at least three non-burning PM2.5 sources (e.g., residential biomass, industrial, vehicular).
- Sensitivity analysis showing how attribution percentage changes under alternative methodology assumptions.
- Replication status.

## Health-Impact Requirements

No respiratory health claim linked to agricultural burning may be merged without:

- Confound control documentation for co-occurring seasonal pollution sources (Diwali fireworks, winter inversion, diesel generators).
- Hospital data limitations (health-seeking behavior, capacity constraints, case definition).
- Explicit statement that correlation does not establish causation for burning-specific attribution.
- Non-use case documentation (not for individual farmer penalties, not for blanket ban justification).

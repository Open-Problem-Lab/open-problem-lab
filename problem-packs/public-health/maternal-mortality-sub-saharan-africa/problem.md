# Maternal Mortality Facility Delivery And Emergency Obstetric Care Access In Sub-Saharan Africa

## Problem Statement

Every two minutes, a woman dies from pregnancy or childbirth complications. Sub-Saharan Africa carries two-thirds of the global burden. The interventions work — skilled birth attendance, emergency obstetric care, blood transfusion — but they only work if a woman can reach a functional facility in time. Travel-time maps exist from Malaria Atlas Project and others, but connecting them to verified EmOC facility readiness data at sub-national scale remains underdeveloped.

> Which sub-national areas in Sub-Saharan Africa have the highest evidence-backed gap between maternal mortality risk and emergency obstetric care access?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO estimates 287,000 maternal deaths globally in 2020, with Sub-Saharan Africa accounting for approximately 70 percent. Lifetime risk exceeds 1 in 40 in the highest-burden countries.
- Verified fact: The three delays model identifies delay in deciding to seek care, delay in reaching a facility, and delay in receiving adequate treatment — with the second and third delays directly measurable from travel-time and facility-readiness data.
- Verified fact: The 2018 Lancet Commission on High-Quality Health Systems found that facility delivery rates have increased but that many facilities classified as providing emergency obstetric care lack blood transfusion, cesarean section, or magnesium sulfate.
- Verified fact: Malaria Atlas Project travel-time maps at 1km resolution exist for most of Sub-Saharan Africa, enabling population-level access estimation.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether health facility registry data on EmOC signal functions is current and accurate enough for access modeling.
- How seasonal road conditions affect travel-time estimates.
- Whether access maps change actual ambulance placement or referral protocol decisions.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, EmOC facility readiness mapping, travel-time access analysis, gap identification, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national maternal health access gap analysis for at least two countries.

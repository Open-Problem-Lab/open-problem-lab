# Avoidable Blindness And Cataract Surgical Coverage In LMICs

## Problem Statement

2.2 billion people have vision impairment. 1 billion cases are preventable or unaddressed. Cataract causes 50 percent of blindness in LMICs, yet surgical coverage is below 50 percent. RAAB surveys measure cataract surgical coverage and blindness prevalence at district level, but surveys are infrequent. Linking RAAB data to eye-health workforce maps and surgical-facility locations would identify where the avoidable-blindness burden is highest and surgical access is lowest.

> Which sub-national areas in LMICs have the lowest evidence-backed cataract surgical coverage and highest avoidable blindness prevalence?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO World Report on Vision estimates 2.2 billion people with vision impairment, 1 billion preventable or unaddressed, with cataract causing 50 percent of blindness in LMICs.
- Verified fact: RAAB surveys provide gold-standard sub-national blindness prevalence and cataract surgical coverage data in over 40 countries, but surveys are conducted every 5-10 years.
- Verified fact: Cataract surgical coverage is below 50 percent in most low-income countries and below 30 percent in several Sahel and Central African countries.
- Verified fact: Eye-health workforce density is under 1 ophthalmologist per 1 million population in most of Sub-Saharan Africa, concentrated in capital cities.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether RAAB data can be extrapolated between surveys using demographic and health-system proxies. Whether CSC gap maps change eye-health program investment.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, RAAB data compilation, workforce-facility mapping, CSC gap analysis, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national CSC gap analysis for at least two LMICs.

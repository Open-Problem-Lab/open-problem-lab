## Evidence Notes

### WHO World Report on Vision 2019

Use this source for the global vision-impairment burden baseline — 2.2 billion people with vision impairment, of whom 1 billion cases are preventable or unaddressed. Cataract accounts for approximately 50 percent of blindness in LMICs. Cataract surgical coverage (CSC) — the proportion of people with operable cataract who have received surgery — is below 50 percent in most low-income countries and below 30 percent in several Sahel and Central African countries.

The estimates are modeled from RAAB (Rapid Assessment of Avoidable Blindness) surveys conducted in approximately 40 countries, but survey frequency is only every 5-10 years. Do not use the global 2.2 billion figure as a substitute for sub-national CSC data. Where RAAB surveys are older than 5 years, the CSC estimate should be flagged as potentially outdated.

### RAAB Survey Methodology Context

RAAB surveys use population-based sampling with visual acuity measurement and lens examination to estimate blindness prevalence and cataract surgical coverage at district level. RAAB is the gold standard for avoidable-blindness measurement in LMICs, but surveys are infrequent, and CSC estimates depend on population denominators that may be outdated. Any CSC gap analysis for districts without recent RAAB surveys must include explicit caveats about the age of the underlying data.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

## Evidence Notes

### WHO Nurturing Care 2023

Use this source for the global ECD risk baseline. The 250 million figure is a modeled estimate from poverty, malnutrition, and stimulation-deprivation prevalence — not a direct developmental-delay measurement. ECD instruments vary across countries. Intervention-coverage data is sparse at sub-national scale. Always document these limitations in sub-national analyses.

### MICS ECDI Measurement

Use MICS ECDI as the primary ECD-outcome data source with critical caveats: instruments have been revised across survey rounds, limiting temporal comparability. Country coverage is limited. Sub-national sample sizes may be insufficient for district-level analysis. ECDI scores are parent-reported, not direct child assessment — reporting bias varies across cultural contexts. Always document instrument-comparability limitations.

### Preschool Enrollment Low Income

Use this source for preschool-coverage context. The below-20-percent figure is a national average masking enormous sub-national variation. Urban-rural gaps are documented in some countries but may not generalize. Enrollment does not capture quality — a child in a program with no stimulation receives limited benefit. Always document the quality-gap and sub-national-variation limitations.

### Parenting Intervention Effects

Use this source for the strongest available evidence on parenting-program effectiveness. The 0.3-0.5 SD effect is pooled across diverse designs and contexts. Publication bias cannot be excluded. Sustainability beyond program period is rarely assessed. Coverage below 5 percent means evidence comes from small-scale trials that may not replicate at scale. Always present with heterogeneity and sustainability caveats.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

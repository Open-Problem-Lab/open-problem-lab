# Childhood Vaccination Coverage Equity Gaps In LMICs

## Problem Statement

Immunization prevents 4 million deaths annually — one of the most cost-effective health interventions. Yet 20 million children remain unvaccinated or under-vaccinated, concentrated in the poorest wealth quintiles and remote districts. IA2030 targets zero-dose reduction, but measuring sub-national equity gaps — where zero-dose children live by wealth, geography, and access barriers — is underdeveloped.

> Which sub-national areas and population groups have the highest evidence-backed vaccination coverage equity gaps?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: WHO/UNICEF estimates 20 million children did not receive three doses of DTP in 2023, with 60 percent in ten countries and the majority in the poorest wealth quintile and remote rural areas.
- Verified fact: DHS and MICS surveys provide sub-national vaccination coverage with wealth-quintile disaggregation, enabling equity-gap measurement at district level.
- Verified fact: The Immunization Agenda 2030 targets 50 percent reduction in zero-dose children, but measurement requires survey data that is collected every 3-5 years.
- Verified fact: Zero-dose children are disproportionately in urban slums, conflict zones, and remote rural areas with poor health-facility access.

## Uncertain Areas

Whether survey-based equity gaps can be supplemented with routine HMIS data for more frequent measurement. Whether equity-gap maps change immunization outreach strategy.

## Initial Scope

Evidence inventory, coverage data compilation, equity-gap mapping, zero-dose identification, red-team review, field-reality review.

## Done Condition For V0

One reviewed and replicated sub-national vaccination equity gap analysis for at least two LMICs.

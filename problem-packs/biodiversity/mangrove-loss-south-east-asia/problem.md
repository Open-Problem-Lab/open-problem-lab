# Mangrove Deforestation And Coastal Protection Loss In Southeast Asia

## Problem Statement

Southeast Asia has lost 30 percent of mangroves since 1980 — cleared for shrimp ponds and coastal development. Mangroves reduce storm-surge height by 30-70 percent and sustain fisheries. Global Mangrove Watch provides annual 25m-resolution maps. But linking deforestation to quantified coastal-protection service loss at sub-national scale is the missing analysis.

> Which coastal areas in Southeast Asia have lost the most mangrove-based storm-surge protection, and where are the remaining mangroves most at risk?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: Global Mangrove Watch estimates Southeast Asia has lost over 30 percent of mangroves since 1980, primarily to aquaculture conversion, with Indonesia, Myanmar, and Thailand having the highest absolute losses.
- Verified fact: Mangroves reduce storm-surge height by 30-70 percent and wave energy by 50-90 percent, with protection value varying by mangrove width, density, and species composition.
- Verified fact: A 2020 Nature study valued global mangrove ecosystem services at over $1.6 trillion, with coastal protection as the largest component.

One reviewed and replicated mangrove-loss coastal-protection impact analysis for at least two Southeast Asian countries.

# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. Keep quantitative outputs reproducible.
5. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What health-burden or adoption-barrier claim is being made?
2. What household survey or health data supports it?
3. What method produced the estimate?
4. What would make the adoption barrier analysis wrong?
5. Who could misuse a barrier map to justify excluding communities from clean cooking programs?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether LPG adoption would be good — it likely would. Ask whether a named clean cooking program manager in a named country would use this output to change their distribution, subsidy, or community engagement strategy, and what would happen if the barrier assessment was wrong. Fuel stacking means modeled health benefits may not materialize — every output must acknowledge this.

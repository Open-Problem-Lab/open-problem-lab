# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. For prevalence work, document country, region, diagnostic criteria, and sample size for every entry.
5. For causal synthesis, rank hypotheses by evidence strength, not by plausibility or advocacy.
6. Mark every causal or intervention claim as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made?
2. What source proves it?
3. What method produced it?
4. What would make it false?
5. Who could misuse it?

For CKDu specifically, add: Can this study distinguish heat-stress from agrochemical mechanisms? If not, has the limitation been stated?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a causal synthesis is impressive. Ask whether it changes a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For CKDu, the named users are national occupational health ministries, nephrology associations, agricultural employer associations, and worker organizations. The misuse risk is attributing CKDu solely to heat to deflect pesticide regulation, blaming workers for not hydrating, or framing the problem as too uncertain for any regulatory action.

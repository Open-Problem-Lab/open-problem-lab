# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. Keep quantitative outputs reproducible.
5. Mark every program-relevant inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What sanitation-coverage claim is being made?
2. What survey or administrative data supports it?
3. What method produced the triangulation or gap estimate?
4. What would make the district-level estimate wrong?
5. Who could misuse a coverage gap map to penalize states or withdraw program funding?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether more toilets would be good — they would. Ask whether a district collector or SBM program manager would use this output to change their toilet-construction or behavior-change strategy, and what would happen if the coverage gap estimate was wrong. Toilets without water, maintenance, or behavior change support are not used — every output must acknowledge this.

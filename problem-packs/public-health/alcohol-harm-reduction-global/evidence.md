## Evidence Notes

### WHO Global Status Report on Alcohol and Health 2024

Use this source for the global alcohol-attributable mortality burden — 3 million deaths annually, 75 percent among men — and the SAFER policy-implementation tracking showing that LMICs have both the lowest policy implementation and the fastest-growing alcohol markets. The policy-implementation data is self-reported by member states and may not reflect actual enforcement. Do not use the SAFER policy-implementation indicator alone as proof of effective alcohol control — it measures existence of policies, not their enforcement or impact on consumption.

### Alcohol Industry and LMIC Market Context

The alcohol industry is aggressively expanding in LMICs through digital marketing, cross-border advertising, and product innovation targeting young consumers — practices that often evade national advertising regulations. STEPS surveys in over 100 countries measure heavy episodic drinking at sub-national scale, but May not capture informal and home-brewed alcohol consumption, which represents a substantial share of total consumption in many LMICs. Any policy-prevalence gap analysis must acknowledge the data gap for informal alcohol.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.

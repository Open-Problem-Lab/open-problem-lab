# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. Keep quantitative outputs reproducible.
5. Mark every inference about extinction risk as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What species-range loss claim is being made?
2. What deforestation data and species-occurrence data support it?
3. What method produced the range-loss estimate?
4. What would make the range-loss estimate wrong?
5. Who could misuse a deforestation-risk map to justify displacement of indigenous communities?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether the forest loss rate looks alarming — it is. Ask whether a conservation manager or protected-area authority would use this output to change a resource-allocation decision, and what would happen if the species-range loss ranking was wrong. The most dangerous output is one that directs conservation resources away from unmeasured but critical habitats toward well-measured but less important ones.
